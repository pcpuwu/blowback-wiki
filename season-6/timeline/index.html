    Blowback Season 6 — Master Timeline (Angola)¶

    A single real-world chronological spine across all 10 episodes. Each per-episode note (s6eNN-*.md) walks its episode in narration order; this file reorders by date. (E#) = the episode(s) that cover it. Sources: s6-sources.md.

    Old Angola, the slave trade & Portuguese colonialism (15th c.–1920s) — E2¶

    • Late 15th c. — the Portuguese reach the centralized Kongo kingdom (whose Manikongo adopts Christianity) and the Ndongo (its ruler's title Ngola names Angola; Queen Nzinga resists). The 1574 charter to Paulo Dias de Novais begins conquest.
    • For 4–5 centuries Angola is "first and foremost an outpost of the slave trade" — ~40% of all enslaved Africans pass through Portuguese hands; the prophetess Kimpa Vita is burned (1706).
    • After Brazil's independence (1822), Portugal builds "a new African empire," fixing Angola's frontiers by the 1920s and extending forced labor (coffee, rubber, diamonds — Diamang as "a state within a state"). Henry Nevinson's A Modern Slavery (1906) triggers a Cadbury cocoa boycott (1909).

    Salazar, apartheid & America's "reluctant uncle" (1920s–1950s) — E2¶

    • 1920s — a coup brings Salazar to power (president by 1932); the Estado Novo makes empire "fundamental" (1930 Colonial Act); a two-tier "citizens vs. natives" system — "a variant of apartheid."
    • WWII — neutral Portugal sells tungsten to Germany and leases the Azores base to the Allies → into NATO (1949) with a secret clause for US aid in Africa.
    • 1948 — South Africa's National Party wins on apartheid; the Korean War cements the US–South Africa tie ("apartheid's reluctant uncle"). 1950s — coffee boom; SACOR finds oil in Cabinda.

    The three nationalisms & the 1961 uprisings (1955–1968) — E2, E3¶

    • 1955 — Holden Roberto takes CIA money; the three movements form abroad: Roberto's FNLA (northern Bakongo, anti-communist), the MPLA (Luanda/Mbundu, led by Agostinho Neto), and (1966) Jonas Savimbi's UNITA (Ovimbundu south). (E2)
    • 1960 — the "Year of Africa." 1961 — the Angolan uprisings: the Feb 4 Luanda prison attack and the March 15 northern revolt, met by a Portuguese terror campaign (~50,000 killed, 300,000 refugees). JFK condemns Portugal but, for the Azores base, supplies napalm. India seizes Goa (Dec 1961). (E3)
    • 1962–67 — Cuba's African internationalism begins (Che in the Congo); the MPLA finds success in the east; Savimbi secretly collaborates with the Portuguese (~1972). Salazar's 1968 stroke → a two-year charade until his 1970 death (Caetano succeeds). (E3)

    The Carnation Revolution & independence (1974–1975) — E4¶

    • April 25, 1974 — the Carnation Revolution topples the Estado Novo; Guinea-Bissau gets quick independence. The Alvor Agreement (Jan 1975) sets Independence Day for Nov 11, 1975 — but the three factions slide into civil war.
    • July 1975 — the CIA's Operation IA/Feature (Kissinger, the 40 Committee); the MPLA seizes Luanda; apartheid South Africa launches Operation Savannah (task forces Zulu and Foxbat, Oct 14) while Mobutu's Zaire backs the FNLA.
    • Cuba answers with Operation Carlota — thousands of combat troops who halt the South African blitz. Nov 11, 1975 — independence; the MPLA holds the capital. Dec 23 — South Africa decides to withdraw; Dec 19 — the US Congress cuts off covert funds.

    Aftermath, Shaba & Kassinga (1976–1978) — E5¶

    • 1976 — the CIA defeat becomes a Washington scandal (Colby's lies; the Tunney/Clark Amendments); the mercenary trials in Luanda; Angola as a Reagan-vs-Ford campaign cudgel. March 27, 1976 — the last South African troops leave; Cubans peak at 36,000.
    • 1977 — Carter takes office; Shaba I (Katangan invasion of Zaire) poisons US-Cuba normalization; the Nito Alves coup attempt (Cubans save Neto); Cuba sends ~12,000 troops to Ethiopia/the Ogaden.
    • May 1978 — Shaba II and Brzezinski's trap (bleed the Cubans via Savimbi); the Kassinga massacre (600+ Namibian refugees killed).

    Total Onslaught, Neto's death & Reagan (1978–1980) — E6¶

    • 1978 — Brzezinski (the "bleeders") routs Vance and Young; South Africa rejects UN Resolution 435 on Namibia; P.W. Botha brings "Total Onslaught/Total Strategy"; Jamba built as Savimbi's base (1979).
    • Sept 1979 — Agostinho Neto dies (cancer); José Eduardo dos Santos succeeds him. The phantom "Soviet brigade" crisis, the Vela nuclear flash (Israel/South Africa), and Mariel (1980) sink Carter; Reagan wins.

    Constructive engagement & the constellation (1981–1984) — E7¶

    • 1981 — Reagan's October Surprise; Chester Crocker's "constructive engagement"/linkage (Cuban exit ⇄ Namibian independence), accepted by South Africa as a sham cover for its regional "constellation." UNITA takes Mavinga (defended May 1981); South Africa's Operation Protea (Aug 1981, US vetoes the UN condemnation).
    • 1983 — Cubans rise to ~30,000; the Kangamba battle; Savimbi's ivory/hostage rackets and the "Red September" witch-burnings (Sept 7).
    • Jan-Feb 1984 — the Lusaka Agreement (South African withdrawal ⇄ SWAPO barred) and the Nkomati Accord.

    Reagan Doctrine, Savimbi's stardom & Iran-Contra (1984–1987) — E8¶

    • Nov 1984 — Reagan's landslide; the Reagan Doctrine of rollback. US opinion turns on apartheid (township revolt, Rosa Parks/Tutu, Mandela's refusal Feb 1985); June 1985 — the Jamba Jamboree (Abramoff); July 1985 — the Clark Amendment repealed.
    • 1985-86 — Operation Second Congress fails; the Black/Manafort/Stone PR campaign, the Oval Office photo-op, Stinger missiles. Angola moves toward the IMF. Oct 1986 — congressional South Africa sanctions over Reagan's veto; Iran-Contra breaks (quietly entangling Angola).

    Cuito Cuanavale & the New York Accords (1987–1988) — E9¶

    • 1987 — the MPLA's Soviet-pushed Mavinga offensive fails, drawing South Africa's Operation Modular into the siege of Cuito Cuanavale.
    • Nov 1987 — Castro surges troops (defying Gorbachev), wins air supremacy, holds Cuito, and drives a southwest front to the Namibian border (joint Cuban-SWAPO operations; the Cahama airfield). June 1988 — Calueque, the psychological watershed.
    • Dec 22, 1988 — the New York Accords: Cuban withdrawal (27 months) ⇄ Namibian independence. Mandela: Cuito "the turning point for the liberation of our continent." Savimbi's PR collapse (Red Scorpion; the Chigunji revelations, March 1989).

    The post-Cold War war, Savimbi's death & after (1991–present) — E10¶

    • May 1991 — the last Cubans leave; the Bicesse Accords. March 1990 — Namibia independent; 1994 — Mandela's South Africa abandons Savimbi.
    • Sept 1992 — Savimbi loses the (free and fair) election and restarts the war of cities (the Huambo siege; diamonds, Executive Outcomes, post-Soviet arms). May 1993 — the US recognizes the MPLA. The Lusaka Protocol (Nov 1994).
    • 1997 — Mobutu falls (Savimbi loses his Zaire base). Feb 22, 2002 — Savimbi killed (Israeli surveillance help); UNITA signs peace two months later — ending Africa's longest civil war (~500,000–1 million dead, ~4 million displaced).
    • 2002–present — the dos Santos crony-capitalist dynasty (Isabel dos Santos); João Lourenço (from 2017) courts both the US and China; the Lobito Corridor and Biden's 2024 visit make Angola a front in the US-China rivalry.
