    Blowback S4E6 — "Ground Zeroes"¶

    🎧 Listen here: Spotify · blowback.show 📄 Read the full transcript

    Hosts: Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · Runtime: ~1:03:00 Thesis: The road to 9/11 (1996–2001) — the Afghan-Arab jihad metastasizing worldwide (Bosnia, the Philippines, Algeria, the Caucasus); bin Laden declaring war from Afghanistan; the embassy and USS Cole bombings; the buried Saudi connections and the spiked intelligence on the hijackers; the US/Taliban pipeline diplomacy running right up to the attacks; and the attacks themselves — used as the trigger for a war already agreed on Sept 10.

    Narration order; [hh:mm:ss] = audio jump point; real dates in bold. Speech-to-text slips corrected (al-Mihdhar ≈ "Midar," al-Hazmi ≈ "Hosmi/Hazmi," Mazar-i-Sharif ≈ "Mazari Sharif," al-Bayoumi ≈ "Bayumi," al-Awlaki ≈ "Aulaki/Laki," Musharraf ≈ "Misharraf").

    Cold open: John O'Neill [00:00:00]¶

    • June 1996 — the Khobar Towers bombing (Dharan) killed 19 US soldiers. FBI counterterror chief John O'Neill found bin Laden's trail "off limits" — Saudis interrogated suspects themselves; "all the clues… can be found in Saudi Arabia," but they'd be ignored. Why? "Only one [reason]: corporate oil interests" — no pressure on the Saudi business partner. O'Neill, obsessed with bin Laden and disillusioned, left the FBI in 2001 to become head of security at the World Trade Center — and died there on 9/11, having run back inside to help. His last view: "The kingdom has much more pressure on us than we have on them." [00:00:00]

    Bin Laden in Afghanistan [00:05:00]¶

    • Early 1998 — ABC's John Miller learned the FBI now believed bin Laden was behind Somalia, the Yusuf plots, and plans to kill Clinton and the Pope. Mullah Omar revealed (early 1997) that bin Laden was a Taliban guest. Bin Laden ran his network through a Yemeni hub — Ahmed al-Hada's house ("the epicenter of bin Laden's war against America"; 221 calls 1996–98); al-Hada's son-in-law was Khalid al-Mihdhar. Miller's ABC team interviewed bin Laden at his Afghan base (soldiers firing AK-47s in the air; bin Laden flanked by Mohammed Atef and Ayman al-Zawahiri): "our battle with the Americans is larger than our battle with the Russians… a black day for America." [00:05:00]

    The jihad goes global [00:09:33]¶

    • The Afghan veterans fanned out to new battlegrounds:
    • Bosnia [00:09:33] — the "cause célèbre of the radical Islamist world"; Al-Kifah recruited from Brooklyn (a convicted terrorist said the Saudi embassy gave him $150,000); fighters who later did the 1993 WTC and 2005 London bombings trained here.
    • The Philippines [00:12:00] — the Abu Sayyaf group (founder a friend of bin Laden) kidnapping and beheading for ransom (~$25M in 2000).
    • Algeria [00:14:00] — the FIS and a decade-long dirty war (~200,000 dead).
    • Azerbaijan & Kosovo [00:15:44] — ex-Iran-Contra player Richard Secord organizing ex-mujahideen; the KLA's ties to al-Qaeda training (unmentioned in mainstream accounts).
    • Libya [00:16:38] — the first Interpol warrant for bin Laden came from Gaddafi's Libya (April 1998) — kept secret because British MI5 had worked with bin Laden's Libyan allies in a 1996 plot to assassinate Gaddafi.

    The Mazar-i-Sharif massacre [00:18:18]¶

    • August 1998 — the Taliban took the northern city of Mazar-i-Sharif and massacred primarily the Hazara minority (house-to-house executions; bodies left to rot for days; eight Iranian diplomats and a journalist also killed — still a live issue with Iran). It got little US attention — overshadowed by the embassy bombings the day before. [00:18:18]

    The embassy bombings [00:20:24]¶

    • Aug 7, 1998 (the 8th anniversary of US troops arriving in Saudi Arabia) — twin truck bombs hit the US embassies in Nairobi (213 dead, ~4,500 injured) and Dar es Salaam (11 dead). The Nairobi site had been scouted in 1993 by Ali Mohamed (Green Beret/FBI informant/CIA asset, whom the Mounties once released after a call to his FBI handler) — bin Laden "pointed to where a truck could go as a suicide bomber." The US claimed Mohamed was a double agent who deceived his handlers. [00:20:24]
    • The response [00:24:46]: a CIA bin Laden analyst told Tenet "you are responsible for those deaths" (a raid plan never recommended to Clinton). Three days after being told bin Laden was responsible, Clinton instead briefed the nation on the Lewinsky affair. The cruise-missile retaliation (Aug 20) — weak intel, the militants warned ahead by the ISI — killed 21, not bin Laden; the second strike hit Sudan's Al-Shifa, a medicine factory misportrayed as a chemical-weapons site. By late 1998 Clinton authorized capturing or killing bin Laden; Alec Station had near-constant eyes on him. [00:24:46]

    Pipeline diplomacy resumes [00:28:57]¶

    • The Unocal project collapsed (the Feminist Majority Foundation, backed by Hillary Clinton, campaigned against it), but "no one lost sight of the enormous opportunities." Feb 1999 — Strobe Talbott met the Taliban (proof of bin Laden's culpability + an extradition request + hints of "economic rewards"). Clinton froze Taliban assets. A planned ISI-assisted commando mission to grab bin Laden was scuttled when Musharraf's 1999 coup pushed out Pakistan's civilian leadership. Late 1999 — the CIA's Alec Station chief revived the alliance with Massoud (the CIA wanted bin Laden; Massoud wanted to run Afghanistan). [00:28:57]

    The hijackers slip in [00:32:19]¶

    • December 1999 — the NSA picked up al-Mihdhar's call about a Kuala Lumpur meeting; the CIA photocopied his passport (a valid US multi-entry visa, destination New York). FBI agent at Alec Station, Doug Miller, tried to alert FBI HQ to put Mihdhar on a watchlist — but the message was spiked by his CIA boss Tom Wilshire ("please hold off… per Tom Wilshire"). The CIA didn't alert the FBI for over a year. Jan 15, 2000 — al-Mihdhar and al-Hazmi landed in Los Angeles. [00:32:19]
    • The Saudi handler [00:36:00]: in LA, Omar al-Bayoumi (connections to the Saudi consulate, salary from the Saudi Defense Ministry) "took them under his wing" — found their San Diego apartment, co-signed the lease, lent rent. The FBI's later "Operation Encore" found Bayoumi part of a network "created, funded, directed, and supported by the Saudi government." Speculation (Richard Clarke): "a possible failed CIA/Saudi spy mission on US soil that went bad." Bayoumi also called the San Diego imam Anwar al-Awlaki (later AQAP leader, killed by a 2011 Obama drone strike). [00:36:00]
    • The USS Cole [00:39:00]: the NSA eavesdropped on Mihdhar/Hazmi for months without passing on their location (they lived in an FBI informant's home, unknown to the FBI). They were in touch with Khalid Sheikh Mohammed. Mihdhar flew home to Yemen (June 2000) and helped with the USS Cole attack — Oct 12, 2000 (17 US sailors killed; the support-team videographer overslept). [00:39:00]

    The 2000 election & ignored warnings [00:43:50]¶

    • The Bush–Gore campaign barely touched terrorism (Bush rejecting being the "world's policeman," disagreeing with Gore on "nation-building"). After the Supreme Court installed Bush, the promised "swift justice" for the Cole never came. Throughout 2001, despite a "steady drumbeat of warnings" (bin Laden in ~36 presidential daily briefings), "there was little appetite for the discussion." Abu Sayyaf beheaded a Californian (June 2001). [00:43:50]

    The final round of pipeline talks [00:48:00]¶

    • Lely Helms spun the diplomatic crisis into opportunity, knowing the new Bush administration was an oil administration (Cheney/Halliburton, Rice/Chevron). The US reopened talks with the Taliban — and even Hekmatyar's party (now merged with bin Laden) — via Christina Rocca (ex-CIA, who'd supervised Stinger deliveries to the mujahideen). Summer 2001 was full of secret diplomacy to push a Taliban–Northern Alliance coalition that would extradite bin Laden ("win the jackpot"). It broke down; in a final Berlin meeting, a US official's ultimatum: "either you accept our offer of a carpet of gold, or we bury you under a carpet of bombs" — the first official threat of military action against Afghanistan in the 21st century. [00:48:00]
    • August 2001 [00:51:00]: Mihdhar was finally put on a CIA watchlist — unused. He bought plane tickets at William Paterson University; Hazmi moved in with Mohamed Atta; on Sept 5, Mihdhar paid for tickets at Dulles in $100 bills. [00:51:00]

    Massoud's assassination & the war already planned [00:52:43]¶

    • Sept 9, 2001 — Massoud was killed by a camera-bomb in a fake interview (a "gift" bin Laden organized for the Taliban); the US agreed to keep his Northern Alliance alive. Sept 10, 2001 — the Bush team agreed to pursue "a covert war to overthrow the Taliban." [00:52:43]

    September 11 [00:54:07]¶

    • The morning of the attacks; the four hijacked flights. At 8:46 the CIA's counterterror chief Cofer Black got word; then a call from his NY officer: "We've been struck." At 9:37 Mihdhar/Hazmi's plane hit the Pentagon; Rumsfeld strode toward the crash site (briefly unlocatable), then issued the directive: "Go massive. Sweep it all up. Things related and not." By 3 PM Tenet confirmed to Bush that al-Qaeda was responsible. [00:54:07]
    • Pakistan's choice [00:59:47]: Musharraf — "America was sure to react violently, like a wounded bear." Armitage's "most undiplomatic statement ever": choose the US or "be prepared to be bombed back to the Stone Age." Pakistan's adopted policy (per Rashid): "first say yes, and later say but." [00:59:47]
    • The war begins [01:01:16]: Bush — "We will make no distinction between the terrorists who committed these acts and those who harbor them." Oct 2 he approved Operation Enduring Freedom; boots on the ground within a week. Alec Station's Richard Blee told a Northern Alliance leader: "This is now much beyond you… This is going to change your country forever… in ways, in scope, in scale that you cannot imagine." [01:01:16]
