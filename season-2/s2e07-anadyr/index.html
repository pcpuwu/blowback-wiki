    S2E8 — We'll All Meet in Hell →

    🎧 Listen here: Spotify · blowback.show 📄 Read the full transcript

    Hosts: Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · Runtime: 1:11:28 Thesis: How the Cuban Missile Crisis began — Khrushchev's gamble to defend Cuba (and balance the US Jupiter missiles) with Operation Anadyr, the secret deployment of Soviet nukes, and the first frantic week of JFK's ExComm, ending with the quarantine speech of Oct 22, 1962.

    Narration order; [hh:mm:ss] = audio jump point; real dates in bold. Obvious speech-to-text slips corrected against context.

    Cold open [00:00:06]¶

    • A 1999 Pentagon declassification: the US had stationed nuclear material in Puerto Rico (1956) and had nuclear assemblies at Guantánamo during the crisis — undercutting the framing of Cuba as the sole nuclear aggressor. [00:00:06]

    Khrushchev's gamble [00:02:26]¶

    • CIA chief McCone had said the quiet part aloud (Aug 1962): "if I were Khrushchev, I'd put offensive missiles in Cuba… how do you like looking down the barrel of a gun?" [00:02:26]
    • After the Bay of Pigs and the visible Mongoose buildup, Khrushchev and Castro expected a real US invasion. In spring/May 1962 Khrushchev conceived of secretly placing Soviet nuclear missiles in Cuba — to deter invasion and to balance the US Jupiters ringing the USSR (Turkey, Italy): a "balance of fear" ("put a hedgehog down the Americans' pants"). He ruled out a first strike ("every idiot can start a war… impossible to win it"), and wanted secrecy until after the Nov midterms. Context: Soviet intel that the US had drawn up a nuclear first-strike plan during the 1961 Berlin standoff. [00:02:26]
    • The offer to Cuba [00:09:50]: a Soviet delegation disguised as an "agricultural mission" (May 1962) pitched the missiles; Castro accepted (to "consolidate the defensive power of the entire socialist camp"). Mikoyan warned the secrecy was a mistake. [00:09:50]

    The plot, and Anadyr [00:14:18]¶

    • Harvey's pared-down assassination team (Verona, Roselli, five poison pills — now also targeting Raúl and Che) was being watched by the FBI; Hoover had leverage (JFK and Giancana shared a mistress, Judith Campbell). [00:14:18]
    • Operation Anadyr [00:15:55]: a masterclass in maskirovka — named for a Siberian river, ~85 ships over ~180 trips, cargo hidden under tractors, captains opening sealed orders mid-ocean. Ultimately 164 nuclear warheads reached Cuba; by Oct 20 eight medium-range missiles were operational. [00:15:55]

    Mongoose escalates (summer 1962) [00:17:45]¶

    • July 14 — a tortured Cuban fisherman (Rodolfo Rosell) washes up near Guantánamo. Aug 20 — JFK approves Mongoose Phase 2 (more sabotage/terror). Aug 22 — CIA poisons stored Cuban sugar in Puerto Rico. Aug 24 — the CIA-funded DRE student group shells a Havana suburb (Miramar) from boats, hoping to hit Castro; their commander Borja went on Meet the Press — the CIA pulled the DRE closer, not away. [00:17:45]

    Keating sounds the alarm [00:30:09]¶

    • Aug 31, 1962 — Senator Kenneth Keating announced a Soviet buildup in Cuba (sourced from McCone, the DRE via Clare Boothe Luce, and Pentagon leaks to Strom Thurmond). The White House, terrified for the midterms, dismissed it (the SAMs were "only for shooting down planes"). Prescott Bush among the hawks; a Sept 20 Senate resolution authorized force if Cuba threatened US security. Khrushchev added tactical nukes and, balancing his messages, told a US official "now we can swat your ass." JFK called up 150,000 reservists (Sept 7). [00:30:09]

    Discovery [00:39:52]¶

    • Oct 4 — the first warheads arrive (the Indigirka). Oct 14 — a U-2 flight (Maj. Heyser) photographs the missile sites; Oct 15 confirmed. Oct 16 — Bundy tells JFK ("he can't do that to me"); RFK: "those sons of bitches Russians" (ironically, the secrecy was meant to spare the Kennedys a midterm headache). JFK convenes ExComm. [00:39:52]

    ExComm's first week [00:44:00]¶

    • From the start the hawks (Taylor, LeMay, Bundy) pushed a surprise airstrike/invasion; McNamara floated diplomacy / blockade / invasion. The legal bind: the Soviet missiles were legal (a sovereign ally's choice), but a military blockade without a declaration of war was flagrantly illegal — and everyone knew it. [00:44:00]
    • Adlai Stevenson was nearly alone in urging JFK to see it from Khrushchev's side — and to trade the Jupiters (Turkey/Italy) and even Guantánamo. Gromyko played dumb in an Oct 18 meeting. The Joint Chiefs' own crisis-week minutes were later destroyed (1974). [00:51:00]
    • Oct 19 — the showdown with the Joint Chiefs: JFK reframed it ("we are also talking about Berlin"); LeMay: "you're in a pretty bad fix," JFK: "you're in it with me." JFK leaned to a blockade rebranded as a "quarantine" (a PR screen for what was legally an act of war). [00:59:55]
    • Oct 20 — the blockade is formally approved (Taylor/Joint Chiefs outvoted; RFK wrangles ExComm; airstrikes held in reserve if the missiles aren't removed). Privately JFK was willing to "sacrifice the Jupiters to maintain the peace." [01:05:36]

    The speech [01:08:00]¶

    • Oct 22, 1962 — JFK's televised address reveals the missiles and the quarantine; the Navy encircles Cuba; readiness goes to DEFCON 2. In Moscow the Presidium braced — Khrushchev: "we were not going to unleash war… this may end in a big war." [01:08:00]
