# Blowback Season 3 — Master Timeline (The Korean War)

> A single **real-world chronological spine** across all 10 episodes. Each per-episode note (`s3eNN-*.md`) walks its episode in *narration* order; this file reorders by date. `(E#)` = the episode(s) that cover it. Sources: [`s3-sources.md`](s3-sources.md).

## Old Korea & the colonial era (10th c.–1945) — *E2*
- **10th–15th c.** — Koryŏ then Chosŏn dynasties unify the peninsula (movable type, an alphabet); a rigid Yangban/Confucian order atop ~30% hereditary slaves.
- **1866** — the **General Sherman** incident; **1871** the "Little War with the Heathen" (~650 Koreans killed). **1876–82** unequal treaties pry Korea open.
- **1894–95** — the **Tonghak** peasant rising → the **First Sino-Japanese War**; Japan takes Taiwan.
- **1893–1902** — America's parallel empire: the **Hawaii** planter coup (the Fosters/Doles), the **Philippine-American War** (>1M dead; military governor **Arthur MacArthur**).
- **1904–05** — the **Russo-Japanese War**; TR brokers the peace and blesses Japan's grip on Korea. **Aug 1910** — Korea becomes a **Japanese colony**.
- **March 1, 1919** — the independence movement (thousands killed). The colony splits Koreans into anti-Japanese **communists** and Washington-looking **nationalists** (Rhee).
- **1931 onward** — Japan's Manchuria invasion sparks guerrilla resistance; **Kim Il-sung** becomes a hunted guerrilla leader. Japan overdevelops the North (steel, chemicals); seeds of the chaebol.
- **1937–45** — Japan invades China → Pearl Harbor (1941); ~200,000 Korean "comfort women"; FDR dies → Truman; Hiroshima/Nagasaki; Japan surrenders **to the US**.

## Liberation & the two occupations (1945–48) — *E3*
- **Aug 14, 1945** — two US colonels (**Dean Rusk** + Bonesteel) pick the **38th parallel** off a National Geographic map. *(E2)*
- **Aug 15, 1945** — Japan surrenders; Koreans form self-governing **people's committees**; the Soviet Red Army liberates the North.
- **Sept 1945** — the **Korean People's Republic** declared; Gen. **Hodge** (the "Blue House") refuses to recognize it. The US South keeps the Japanese-collaborator order (~75% of police ex-colonial); rice "free market" → famine.
- **1946** — Daegu uprising and a 300,000-worker general strike crushed; in the North, Kim's **land reform** (free, no compensation), people's-committee elections, a literacy/women's-rights revolution; the Korean Workers' Party (Aug 1946).
- **1947** — the South outlaws communism; the US Truman Doctrine, National Security Act (CIA), Marshall Plan.

## The "war before the war" (1948–50) — *E4*
- **Aug 15, 1948** — the **Republic of Korea** (Rhee president); **Sept 9, 1948** — the **DPRK** (Kim Il-sung). USSR withdraws troops by end of 1948.
- **March 1947 → April 3, 1948** — the **Jeju** uprising and massacre (Gen. Dean rejects the April 28 peace deal); ~**60,000+** killed (the governor's private figure), half the villages destroyed.
- **Oct 1948** — the **Yeosu** rebellion (ROK units refuse to deploy to Jeju), crushed under US operational control; Rhee's "National Guidance Alliance" concentration/re-education camps.
- **1949** — the **Chinese communist victory** → the US "loss of China" panic; the Soviet A-bomb (Sept); North Korea's China-veteran troops begin returning.
- **Feb 1950** — the **Sino-Soviet Treaty**. **Jan–Feb 1950** — Hiss convicted; **McCarthy's** Wheeling speech.

## The road to June 1950 — *E5*
- **Summer 1949** — South-instigated clashes along the 38th parallel (Kaesong, Ongjin); the North not yet ready (troops still in China). Last Southern assault **Dec 1949**.
- **Winter 1949 → spring 1950** — Kim lobbies Stalin; secret Moscow (March 30–April 25, 1950) and Beijing visits; Stalin gives tacit approval (no Soviet troops), Mao reluctantly backs it (guarding the Formosa plan).
- **Feb 1950** — the Korean aid bill effectively makes unification illegal. **Late May 1950** — ROK elections return moderates; Rhee looks likely to lose power.
- **June 19, 1950** — **Dulles** peers over the parallel, promises aid, meets Rhee (who wants to attack the North). A US military confab in the Pacific; war plan **SL-17** distributed (week of June 18).

## The invasion & the rout (June–Sept 1950) — *E5, E6*
- **June 25, 1950** — the **KPA invades** (the North had lifted its own landmines 48 hrs before); **Seoul falls June 28** (the Han bridge blown with no warning).
- **June 27–30** — Truman commits forces ("**police action**"), no Congress; the UN votes 9–0 (USSR boycotting); the Seventh Fleet to the Taiwan Strait; ground troops by June 30.
- **July 1950** — Rhee's retreat massacres: **No Gun Ri**, **Daejeon** (~7,000), Jeju executions. MacArthur made UN commander (July 8), flies to Formosa (late July). The KPA's advance halts at the **Busan perimeter** (early August).
- **Sept 15, 1950** — the **Inchon landing** (~40,000 vs ~2,000) + breakout from Busan = a pincer; **Seoul retaken ~Sept 28** (claimed Sept 25). *(E6)*
- **Sept 29** — Truman/Acheson/Marshall green-light crossing the parallel and approve **NSC-68**. **Oct 1** — MacArthur's unconditional-surrender proclamation; ROK troops cross the parallel. **Oct 8** — US jets strafe a Soviet airfield near Vladivostok. **Oct 15** — the **Wake Island** meeting. **Oct 24+** — MacArthur defies the buffer zone and drives to the Yalu.

## China enters (Oct–Dec 1950) — *E7*
- **Sept 30** — Zhou Enlai warns (via India) China will fight if the parallel is crossed. The Politburo debates; **Mao decides** (over Lin Biao/Zhou's objections); Stalin's whiplash on air cover, then relents.
- **Oct 18–19, 1950** — the **Chinese Volunteer Army** crosses the Yalu. **First campaign** (Unsan, Oct 26 / Nov 1): US 8th Cavalry battalions fall.
- **Nov 8** — US destroys ~90% of **Sinuiju** as the UN prepares to negotiate. **Nov 24** — MacArthur's "**Home by Christmas**" offensive; the Chinese/NK counterattack two days later.
- **Late Nov–Dec** — the **Chongchon River** and **Chosin Reservoir** battles drive the UN back below the parallel; **Mao Anying** killed by US napalm (Nov 25). Truman talks A-bomb (Attlee rushes over); A-bombs eyed for Guam.
- **Dec 16, 1950** — Truman declares a **national emergency**. NK civilians dig in; daily ROK executions (the Hongseong incident, Dec 15).

## Stalemate, firing, and the air war (1951) — *E8, E9*
- **Christmas Eve 1950** — MacArthur requests permission to use ~30–50 atomic bombs. *(E8)*
- **Third phase** (New Year's 1951) — the communists retake Seoul (briefly); Peng's exhausted army insists on a pause (gets two months). Seoul abandoned/burned by the US ("philosophy of if").
- **Feb 1, 1951** — **UN Resolution 498** brands China the aggressor. Feb — the destruction/blockade of **Wonsan**.
- **March 1951** — MacArthur defies Truman (the Joe Martin letter; his own March 24 ultimatum to China). **April 11, 1951** — Truman **fires MacArthur** (Ridgway takes over); A-bombs quietly shipped to Guam/Okinawa. *(E8)*
- **April–July 1951** — MacArthur's hero's welcome, hearings (LBJ's "enough rope"), and failed political turn. *(E9)*
- **June 23, 1951** — Malik's peace offer (Truman's own terms) rejected. Talks open at **Kaesong** (July 10), repeatedly sabotaged by US attacks — timed partly to protect the **San Francisco Japan treaty** (Sept 1951). *(E9)*
- **Throughout** — the **genocidal air war**: 635,000 tons of bombs + 32,557 tons of napalm; 18 of 22 NK cities ≥50% destroyed; LeMay's "burned down every town… killed off ~20% of the population." Cumings calls it genocide. *(E9)*

## POWs, brainwashing, biowar (1951–53) — *E8, E9*
- **1951** — vast Korean-POW camps (**Koje Island**, ~170,000); the **"voluntary repatriation"** policy (the PSB) breaks the Geneva norm and prolongs the war 18 months. *(E8, E9)*
- **The brainwashing panic** — the word coined (Edward Hunter, Sept 1950); **MK-Ultra**'s precursors (Bluebird, April 1950); Reagan's POW documentary. *(E8)*
- **Biological warfare** — Pak Hon-yong's accusation (May 1951); chicken-feather smallpox; the "plague ship"; the pilots' confessions; the **Needham** report (1952). *(E8)*
- **May–June 1952** — the **Dodd kidnapping** at Koje; the June 10 paratrooper raid (31 killed). *(E9)*
- **Nov 1952** — Eisenhower defeats Stevenson (MacArthur's convention speech tanks). *(E9)*

## Armistice & the long aftermath (1953–2022) — *E9, E10*
- **June 18, 1953** — Rhee releases 25,000 NK POWs to sabotage the truce. **July 27, 1953** — the **armistice** (not a peace treaty); the DMZ created. **2–4 million dead**, most in the North. The Rosenbergs executed (June 19, 1953). *(E9, E10)*
- **1958** — the **US stations nuclear weapons** in the South (escalatory, violating the armistice). *(E10)*
- **1960** — Rhee falls to a student revolt → a flash of reunification hope. **May 16, 1961** — **Park Chung-hee's coup**; a developmental dictatorship; the South in Vietnam. *(E10)*
- **1968** — the Blue House raid, USS **Pueblo** seizure, **Unit 684** mutiny. **1976** — the JSA axe murders (Operation Paul Bunyan). *(E10)*
- **1979** — Park assassinated; **1980** — Chun's coup and the **Gwangju massacre** (US green light). **1987** — democratization begins. *(E10)*
- **1991** — Soviet collapse → North Korean economic chaos; **mid-1990s famine** (~500,000 dead). **1994** — Kim Il-sung dies; **Carter** brokers the **Framework Agreement**. *(E10)*
- **1998–2000** — **Kim Dae-jung's Sunshine Policy**; the June 2000 summit; Albright in Pyongyang; Clinton's summit nearly happens. *(E10)*
- **2001–08** — Bush junks the Framework, names the "**Axis of Evil**," triggering the second nuclear crisis; the six-party talks fail. **Obama** — "strategic patience" + covert sabotage. *(E10)*
- **2018–19** — Moon's revived Sunshine Policy; the Kim–Moon summit and **Panmunjom Declaration**; **Trump–Kim** at Singapore then Hanoi (Trump walks out). **Biden** (2022) — "something in the middle." The unresolved war as "a nail stuck in the flow of history." *(E10)*
