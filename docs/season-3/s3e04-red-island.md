## Cold open `[00:00:02]`
- **1948** — the Republic of Korea is born; Rhee declares a "national emergency" to kill, jail, and torture anyone labeled a **communist** (a label stretched to anyone). A guerrilla war erupts in the South, climaxing on **Jeju Island** — by the end, as many as **100,000 dead**, with the Korean War months away. The DPRK is founded in the North. `[00:00:02]`

## The US Red Scare `[00:03:29]`
- **Henry Wallace**'s 1948 Progressive Party run (to reclaim the New Deal from Truman) was crushed amid anti-communist hysteria (campaigner Florence Gowgiel beaten on the trail). Truman's surprise win embraced anti-communism — which, per Michael Brenes, couldn't deliver health care or strong unions but could funnel federal money to defense and "national security." `[00:03:29]`
- **The UN** `[00:08:00]`: the Security Council's "preponderant power" structure (Prashad — the US sought dominance, the USSR was an umbrella against lawless loophole use). **Dulles** engineered a "Little Assembly" to **circumvent the Soviet veto** and authorize **separate elections in the US zone** that would claim to govern the *whole* peninsula — overriding the North entirely. `[00:08:00]`
- **Hiss/Chambers/Nixon** `[00:13:48]`: the spy case that made Nixon (the CIA fed him info via Dewey and Allen Dulles). `[00:13:48]`

## Jeju — "Red Island" `[00:15:00]`
- Jeju was a self-sufficient, communal island the US initially left alone ("a truly communal area… without much Comintern influence"). But Rhee's growing police state moved to crush it. **March 1, 1947** — police fired on a demonstration → a general strike (≈**75% of the occupation's Korean workers** joined) → nationwide. **April 3, 1948** — 350 rebels rose against the island's police state; a **April 28 peace deal was rejected by US Gen. Dean**, who continued the suppression to ensure the ROK's founding. `[00:15:00]`
- **The massacre** `[00:21:00]`: scorched earth, the **Northwest Youth** terrorist gangs, torture, public executions. The guerrillas (the *Inmin'gun*, ~3–4k, old Japanese arms) were ground down by spring 1949. Toll: the governor privately said **~60,000 died** (one in five or six islanders), ~40,000 fled to Japan, half the villages destroyed. **The US authored the counterinsurgency** — the "omniscient gazer" providing weapons and advisers while placing Koreans on the front line (a recreation of the Japanese colonial model). The **NYT** reported it as a Soviet plot (fabricated Russian submarines/flags); US/police violence was ignored. `[00:21:00]`

## Yeosu & the camps `[00:31:00]`
- **Oct 1948** — ROK regiments **refused to deploy to Jeju**, sparking the **Yeosu rebellion**: people's committees restored, the DPRK flag flown, demands to abolish the 38th parallel and reunify. The crackdown ran under **US operational control** (C-47s, advisers with every ROK unit). Tool of the trade: **concentration camps** — Rhee's "National Guidance Alliance" processed ~3,000 prisoners/week for "re-education." `[00:31:00]`

## Rhee installed; "the loss of China" `[00:37:00]`
- A CIA report admitted the South couldn't survive without US troops and that the **right wing** (the collaborator class that "monopolizes the native wealth and education") produced only demagogues like Rhee. **July 1948** — Rhee won the runoff, inaugurated in August (a fascist-gang chief as his first PM). `[00:37:00]`
- **1949** `[00:42:00]`: the **Chinese communist victory** (Mao: "the Chinese people have stood up") → the "loss of China" panic. Truman's inaugural (Wallace: "closer to a declaration of war"); NATO. The **Soviet A-bomb** (Sept 1949) → Truman's H-bomb decision. Nixon's anti-socialist Senate run. The **Sino-Soviet Treaty** (Feb 1950 — mutual defense; Mao wanted terms "both beautiful and tasty"). `[00:42:00]`
- **McCarthy** `[00:52:00]`: Hiss convicted (Jan 1950); McCarthy's **Wheeling speech** (Feb 1950) — the "Comicrat party," blaming the "loss of China" on a State Dept spy ring; even Democrats joined the hunt. `[00:52:00]`

## The silence before the storm `[00:56:00]`
- Through 1948–March 1950, **Rhee openly threatened to invade the North** ("capture Pyongyang in three days," "ready and waiting to invade" — restrained, he said, only by the Americans). Then, that summer of 1950, "all of a sudden, complete silence." `[00:56:00]`
