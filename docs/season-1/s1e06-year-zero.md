> **Hosts:** Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · **Runtime:** 1:03:55
> **Thesis:** The **first year of the occupation** (mid-2003 → mid-2004) — how the US, driven by free-market ideology and stunning unpreparedness, took a country it had already pulverized and made it catastrophically worse. The title invokes the Khmer Rouge's "Year Zero": tear down every existing institution. (Lenin: "there are decades where nothing happens, and weeks where decades happen.")
>
> _Narration order; `[hh:mm:ss]` = audio jump point; real dates in **bold**._

## Cold open: the green-zone cats `[00:00:00]`
- Uday's private zoo animals scattered during the invasion; occupation staff adopted the stray kittens — until **Halliburton** managers had Marines (then Filipino contract workers) hunt and kill them. A small, telling parable of the occupation's priorities. `[00:00:00]`

## "Freedom" = economic freedom `[00:05:02]`
- "Freedom" became an incantation (a general on helicopters keeping kids awake: "that's the sound of freedom"). Its core meaning was **economic**: Bremer's stated top priority was "the first real free-market economy in the Arab world" — corporatize and privatize. `[00:05:02]`
- **The Green Zone** `[00:06:58]`: 4 sq miles around Saddam's old **Republican Palace** (where Rumsfeld had shaken Saddam's hand in the 80s) — burger joints, a pool, WWII-propaganda posters, staffed largely by young Republican grunts en route to the 2004 Bush campaign. "Once inside, you weren't in Iraq anymore." `[00:06:58]`

## Outside the wall `[00:10:30]`
- Post-invasion Iraq: **electricity at ~4% of pre-war**, no clean water, no social services, mass looting, 150,000 foreign troops raiding homes. Rumsfeld's responses: "stuff happens," "freedom's untidy," and griping about news coverage. `[00:10:30]`
- Killings: a 15-year-old shot waving a white flag in **Thuluya**; a disabled 19-year-old beaten; **Operation Peninsula Strike**; and the **Fallujah massacre (April 2003)** — troops fired on ~1,000 unarmed protesters, killing at least 20, seeding the Sunni insurgency. Iraqi voice **Ra'ed**: the initial goodwill "started wearing off almost immediately." `[00:11:01]`

## Unpreparedness: ORHA & Jay Garner `[00:14:38]`
- Post-war planning went to **Doug Feith**'s Defense Department. The first governing body, **ORHA**, was led by placeholder ex-general **Jay Garner** ("Glengarry Glen Ross" energy). Garner asked Rumsfeld for billions more in reconstruction funds; Rumsfeld: "**if you think we're spending our money on that, you're wrong.**" Colonel Paul Hughes: "we don't owe the Iraqis anything. We're giving them their freedom." Appointees googled their ministries and posted on message boards for advice; watched their ministries get looted on TV ("there goes yours, there goes mine"). The **one ministry protected from day one: oil.** `[00:14:38]`
- **The electrical grid** `[00:18:14]`: already degraded by war + sanctions, then knocked to ~4%. The US would spend over a year just trying to get *back* to Saddam-era levels — Iraqis remembered "say what you want about Saddam, he got the power back on." `[00:18:14]`

## The WMD that weren't `[00:23:28]`
- The **Iraq Survey Group** under **David Kay** went hunting (June 2003); found the mobile labs, tunnels, and tubes were "absolute shite." Cheney pushed Kay toward **Manucher Ghorbanifar** — an Iran-Contra fabricator the CIA had issued a burn notice on — whose "source" wanted $2M upfront. Kay's **Oct 2003** update: a "no-show." Powell (the "reluctant warrior") was furious Kay wouldn't spin it better. Bush's **Jan 2004** State of the Union downgraded "WMD" to "weapons of mass destruction-**related program activities**." Kay resigned: "**I don't believe they existed.**" `[00:23:28]`

## The Viceroy: Paul Bremer & the CPA `[00:29:01]`
- The shortlist for "viceroy" included Giuliani, Bill Weld, Bob Dole, Larry Summers, Wolfowitz. The pick: **Paul "Jerry" Bremer** (May 2003) — a Kissinger Associates alumnus (from the era it brokered deals *with Saddam*), Blackwater security detail, a Halliburton-furnished villa, Giuliani's *Leadership* on his shelf. He created a toothless **Iraqi Governing Council** (July) and ran the **Coalition Provisional Authority (CPA)** himself. `[00:29:01]`
- **Economic shock therapy** `[00:34:01]`: Bremer promised a "wrenching, painful process… as in Eastern Europe after the Berlin Wall" (the hosts: which produced the largest peacetime drop in life expectancy on record). Foreign-investment law (letting multinationals buy former state enterprises — pushed hard by **Colin Powell** over IGC skepticism), slashed tariffs, gutted the top tax bracket — no plan for the resulting unemployment. **The "Neocon Children's Brigade"**: a 24-year-old Yale grad with no finance background set to rebuild the stock exchange; Heritage Foundation applicants routed straight to Baghdad to write Iraq's budgets. (Laid-off vegetable-oil-factory workers kidnapped and shot their boss.) `[00:34:01]`

## The two fatal orders `[00:39:39]`
- **De-Ba'athification (CPA Order 1)** `[00:39:39]` — born in a meeting with Chalabi; framed as "denazification." But by 2003 the Ba'ath was "just Saddamism," party membership often as shallow as US party registration (Ra'ed: his architecture-dept head fired on the spot). In reality it became an **unemployment program for tens of thousands** — including doctors and engineers needed for reconstruction — plus looting and score-settling. `[00:39:39]`
- **Disbanding the army (CPA Order 2)** `[00:42:34]` — sent ~**500,000 armed, trained men** home with no jobs and not even the promised $20 emergency payment. "As close as you could get to a policy to **create your own insurgency.**" `[00:42:34]`

## The business of occupation `[00:44:34]`
- Contractors: Bechtel, Halliburton, Blackwater, GE. **Iraqi TV** was so inadequate (vs. Al-Jazeera) it was nicknamed "the Lebanese cooking channel." **SAIC** (an Iran-Contra guy who bought white Hummers). **Custer Battles** — fake invoices, a **162% markup** ($4M of work billed near $10M) — banned from future contracts after already collecting $100M+. **Viktor Bout** (the *Lord of War* arms dealer) supplied the US Army while on the UN's most-wanted list. `[00:44:34]`
- **Chalabi comes home** `[00:48:36]` — steps off the plane with the "Free Iraqi Forces" (desperadoes; a general: "check your wallets"); they defecated everywhere, may have hijacked a $150M cash truck; Chalabi handed cronies/family the contracts (army equipment, southern cell-phone monopoly), and his men shot up a former associate's office. The polished aristocrat became "the black hand in *Godfather II*." His PR firm **BKSH** won a **PR Week award** for the war propaganda. `[00:48:36]`
- **Bernard Kerik** `[00:53:14]` — Giuliani's ex-NYPD commissioner, made **interior minister of Iraq**; his lone "accomplishment" was a corrupt death squad ("Ibrahim's Rangers"); he later pleaded guilty (2009) to tax evasion over a loan from an Israeli billionaire. `[00:53:14]`

## December 2003: two "victories" `[00:55:53]`
- **Gaddafi** "handed over" his (nearly nonexistent) WMD program to rebrand as a war-on-terror partner. `[00:55:53]`
- **Dec 13, 2003** — **Saddam captured** in a spider hole near Tikrit. "Ladies and gentlemen, we got him." `[00:56:24]`

## But the insurgency grows `[00:57:36]`
- Saddam's capture **didn't slow the violence** — undercutting the whole theory that Ba'athist holdovers were the cause (captured intel showed remaining Ba'athists were focused on looting, not the broad uprising). De-Ba'athification fixed nothing. `[00:57:36]`
- **Bremer leaves June 2004** (handover to an interim government). Ledger: electricity only back to ~pre-war, reconstruction employed just **15,000** Iraqis, the new army had **<4,000** soldiers, **$17B** in reconstruction funds went missing, and the oil industry stayed (loosely) in Iraqi state hands. Attacks climbed: **750/month (Sept 2003) → 1,000 (Oct) → 3,000 (summer 2004).** A CPA spokesman: "off the record, Paris is burning; on the record, stability is returning." `[00:58:23]`
- **The forbidden word** `[01:01:33]`: at an NSC meeting a CIA chief said "insurgency"; Rumsfeld balked, and Bush ordered nobody in the cabinet to use the word — because it would shatter the war's narrative and evoke **Vietnam**. (Rumsfeld, at a podium, claims to have looked "insurgency" up in the dictionary.) `[01:01:33]`
