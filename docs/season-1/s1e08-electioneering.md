# Blowback S1E8 — "Electioneering"

> 📄 **[Read the full transcript](transcripts/s1e08-electioneering.md)**


> **Hosts:** Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · **Runtime:** 59:09
> **Thesis:** The two election years that decided Iraq's fate — the **2004 US election** (run on Iraq, with a pro-war Democrat) and the three-stage **2005 Iraqi elections** — plus the Bush-administration infighting (Rice vs. Rumsfeld) that left the occupation without a coherent strategy as it slid toward civil war.
>

## Cold open: "Bush Was Right" `[00:00:15]`
- The episode opens with the Right Brothers' pro-Bush anthem "Bush Was Right" — the triumphalist mood of "everything's going great" before the questions about Iraq's future begin. `[00:00:15]`

## The 2004 US election `[00:02:51]`
- **2004 was about Iraq** — but the big problem: **John Kerry had voted for the war**, so his campaign couldn't be about *whether* to fight it, only that he'd "do the Iraq War better." ("I'm John Kerry, and I'm reporting for duty.") `[00:02:51]`
- The primary: Howard Dean flamed out, Sharpton briefly led, the media loved Edwards, but Kerry ran away with it after Iowa. His pitch was *personality contrast* (the smart one vs. the bumpkin) and heavy **God-talk** (the would-be Catholic Kennedy heir). `[00:04:20]`
- Crucially, Bush was still ~50% and **Iraq wasn't yet seen as a "mistake"** — the Gallup line crosses 50% only *months after* the election; Americans still saw a "fight for freedom," not an occupation. `[00:07:10]`
- Bush's tactics: a **9/11-imagery attack ad** (flag-draped body from the WTC) that outraged victims' families; the "**flip-flopper**" line ("I voted for the $87 billion before I voted against it"); and the **Swift Boat Veterans for Truth** (funded largely by oil baron T. Boone Pickens) smearing Kerry's war record. `[00:09:43]`
- **Election day** `[00:11:54]`: early exit polls (wrong) suggested Kerry won; he lost. (The hosts: "Google Ohio voting machines 2004.") War-on-terror wedges helped — the color-coded threat alerts (Tom Ridge later said he was pressured to raise the level pre-election), an **FDA "al-Qaeda may poison imported drugs" scare** timed to a Kerry drug-pricing attack (Aug 11, 2004), and an **October bin Laden video** (which CIA analysts later said *helped* Bush). `[00:11:54]`
- **Feb 2005** — second inaugural, all freedom rhetoric (speechwriter Michael Gerson got so worked up writing it he had a heart attack). Post-election shakeup: **Rice → Secretary of State**, **Powell quietly leaves.** `[00:15:39]`

## The 2005 Iraqi elections: the three-stage plan `[00:18:22]`
- The plan: **Jan 2005** → elect a parliament; **Oct 2005** → ratify a constitution; **Dec 2005** → elect the real government (the iconic purple-ink-finger photo op). The Bush administration *needed* this win as the insurgency worsened (suicide-bomb deaths peaked at **200+/month in Feb 2005**, often at polling routes). `[00:18:22]`
- Allawi lied that "**14 of 18 provinces** are completely safe" (a number echoing the post-Gulf-War uprising); Wolfowitz/Rice/Rumsfeld were privately told things were bad. The push for elections came from **Ayatollah Sistani** (a Shia power-play — proving the moderate clerics were the US's only workable partner). Bush was warned elections could **provoke civil war** (Sunnis too disenfranchised to accept them). The clear winners were the **Kurds** (already autonomous, US darlings). `[00:21:23]`
- **Jan 30, 2005 results** `[00:25:29]`: the **United Iraqi Alliance** (Sistani's 22-party Shia bloc) — 48%; the **Kurdish bloc** (Talabani) — 26%; **Allawi** (the US pick) — a mere 14% (a recurring theme: American favorites aren't Iraqi favorites). The UIA fell short of a majority, so it allied with the Kurds and picked the PM. `[00:25:29]`

## Chalabi's collapse `[00:27:49]`
- Chalabi ran for PM (his own newspaper promoting him), down to a two-man race vs. **Jaafari**, "making deals the Chalabi way" — but **nobody wanted him**, tarred by his ties to both the Americans *and* Iran. **May 2004** — Iraqi police (backed by US troops) had **raided his compound** as the **Iran double-agent** story broke (he'd reportedly helped Iran break US codes). He did a **heel turn** (anti-American, cozying up to al-Sadr). He withdrew, got consolation prizes (deputy PM + **acting oil minister**) — and on the day of his confirmation, the Trade Bank of Iraq (run by his nephew) announced a deal with his **credit-card-fraud company**. `[00:27:49]`
- **Jaafari** became PM (April). The Sunni boycott was near-total (single-digit turnout in Anbar) — the elections served **American political priorities**, not Iraqi legitimacy. `[00:32:56]`

## "Clear, hold, build" — and the Rice–Rumsfeld feud `[00:35:02]`
- Rice's strategy slogan **"clear, hold, build"** came from her deputy Philip Zelikow (lifted from the Vietnam book *A Better War*'s "clear and hold," with "build" added for PR). It implied a *long* American commitment — which clashed with **Rumsfeld**'s do-it-cheap **"Vietnamization"** (hand it to Iraqi forces fast). `[00:35:02]`
- The feud was also petty: Rumsfeld saw Rice as a threat. Per Gellman's *Angler*, after Rumsfeld repeatedly dodged her meetings (sending Wolfowitz instead) and cut her out of the detention-policy discussions, **Rice teared up and walked out** ("we will talk about this again"). The administration was doing bureaucratic infighting when it should have had a strategy. `[00:39:39]`

## The mood turns `[00:42:43]`
- **Cheney** on Larry King: the insurgency is "in its **last throes**." Bush became obsessed with **body counts** (a Vietnam/McNamara parallel; the desk drawer of X'd-out photos). `[00:42:43]`
- **Aug 2005** — for the first time, a consistent **majority of Americans call the invasion a mistake.** Kissinger gave Gerson his old "**salted peanuts**" memo (withdrawing troops only makes the public demand more). Rumsfeld rejected any withdrawal **deadline** ("a lifeline to terrorists"). `[00:47:57]`
- **Oct 2005** — the constitution passed (Shia yes, Sunni largely no). Friedman's spin: success is "a document everybody is equally dissatisfied with." `[00:49:56]`

## December 2005: the real government `[00:53:35]`
- **Dec 15, 2005** — Chalabi, now running alone, got **less than 1%** (8,000 Baghdad votes) and "instantly evaporated" — poetic justice for the man who'd helped engineer the invasion to rule a 1950s Iraq that no longer existed. The **United Iraqi Alliance** won again. `[00:53:35]`
- The PM that emerged: **Nouri al-Maliki** — and the deal that put him there was reportedly negotiated by **Qasem Soleimani** (Iran), foreshadowing the next crisis. `[00:54:08]`
- **Cliffhanger:** next episode → the Iraqi **civil war**, officials finally facing consequences, and the strategy to (maybe) get out. `[00:55:32]`
