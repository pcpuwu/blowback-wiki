# Blowback Season 1 — Master Timeline (The Iraq War)

> A single **real-world chronological spine** across all 10 episodes. Each per-episode note (`s1eNN-*.md`) walks its episode in *narration* order; this file reorders everything by date so you can see "what happened when" end-to-end. `(E#)` = the episode(s) that cover it. Sources for every claim live in [`s1-sources.md`](s1-sources.md).

## British Iraq & the oil order (1920–1950s) — *E1*
- **1920** — Churchill favors poison gas "against uncivilized tribes"; Britain bombs an Iraqi uprising.
- **1921** — Britain installs **King Faisal I** via a 96%, single-candidate "election."
- **~pre-WWI** — Churchill brokers a secret oil deal (→ BP) and converts the Royal Navy coal→oil (also a labor-control move).
- **early 20th c.** — Western firms form the **Iraq Petroleum Company** — zero shares to Iraq.
- **after WWII** — US fixes on the USSR; eyes Iraq as a Cold War bulwark. By 1958 Iraq is impoverished (infant mortality ~250/1,000) despite the oil wealth flowing abroad.

## Revolution, the Ba'ath, and the CIA (1958–1979) — *E1*
- **1957** — Saddam joins the **Ba'ath Party** (anti-communist).
- **July 1958** — Revolution: **Gen. Qasim** topples the British-backed monarchy; PM Nuri al-Said killed.
- **Oct 7, 1959** — Saddam in a botched assassination attempt on Qasim; flees to Cairo (reportedly contacts the CIA).
- **Spring 1962** — JFK asks the CIA to plot Qasim's overthrow (poisoned-handkerchief plot).
- **Feb 1963** — Ba'ath coup executes Qasim on live TV; anti-communist purge (~5,000 killed); US recognizes the regime and ships arms. ("We came to power on a CIA train.")
- **1968** — Ba'ath retakes power; Nixon & Kissinger rise in the US.
- **1970s** — Kissinger's Iran/Iraq/Kurds triangulation; a successful Ba'ath welfare state; Saddam nationalizes Iraqi oil.
- **early 1975** — **Algiers Agreement** (Saddam + the Shah, US-mediated) abandons the Kurds.
- **July 1979** — Saddam seizes full power via the videotaped **Ba'ath Party Purge**.

## The Iran–Iraq War (1979–1988) — *E1*
- **Jan 16, 1979** — Shah flees Iran; Islamic Revolution; Khomeini returns.
- **Sept 22, 1980** — Saddam invades Iran (Shatt al-Arab pretext); UN blocks condemnation.
- **June 1981** — Israel bombs the **Osirak** reactor; only then do the US/USSR re-engage Baghdad.
- **1982** — US removes Iraq from the terror-sponsors list.
- **1983–84** — Iraq begins chemical attacks; **Rumsfeld's two visits** to Baghdad; a March 1984 UN report confirms CW use — US restores relations anyway (and supplies targeting intel + precursors).
- **1987** — Reagan: "An Iranian victory is unacceptable"; US-enabled sarin attacks; the **Anfal** genocide of the Kurds begins (50–100k dead). **May 1987** — Iraqi missiles hit the **USS Stark** (37 US dead); no consequences.
- **March 16, 1988** — **Halabja** gas attack (3–5k dead).
- **July 3, 1988** — **USS Vincennes** downs **Iran Air 655** (290 dead); GHW Bush: "I will never apologize for the United States."
- **Summer 1988** — War ends (~180k Iraqi + ~500k Iranian dead; Iraq ~$100B in debt).

## Gulf War & the sanctions decade (1989–2000) — *E2*
- **Summer 1989** — Chalabi flees Jordan after the **Petra Bank** fraud collapse.
- **1990** — GHW Bush faces the "peace dividend"; US/UK/French firms still arm Saddam (Kissinger Associates brokering).
- **July 1990** — Ambassador **April Glaspie** signals the US has "no position" on the Kuwait border.
- **Aug 2, 1990** — Iraq invades Kuwait → Desert Shield; **UN Resolution 661** sanctions; coalition assembled via bribes/threats; the fabricated **Nayirah** incubator testimony (Hill & Knowlton).
- **Jan 17, 1991** — **Desert Storm**: baby-food factory, depleted uranium, the **highway of death**, the **Amiriyah shelter** (400+ dead); infrastructure deliberately destroyed for "post-war leverage" (electricity→4%, water→5%).
- **End Feb 1991** — War ends; Bush incites then abandons the uprising (Saddam loses 14/18 provinces, retakes them); northern no-fly zone.
- **1990s** — UN weapons inspections co-opted by the CIA; sanctions re-defined to last "until Saddam is gone"; **Madeleine Albright**: the price (≈500k dead children) "is worth it"; CIA/Allawi bombings kill civilians; the fake 1993 plot to kill GHW Bush.
- **1998** — the **Iraq Liberation Act** makes regime change US policy; Chalabi's INC funded; PR firm **BKSH** (ex–Black, Manafort, Stone & Kelly).

## 9/11 → the case for war (2001–2002) — *E3, E5*
- **2001** — US-Taliban relations; the **Al-Shifa** strike "dress rehearsal."
- **Sept 11, 2001**; **Sept 12** — Woolsey points at Saddam. The "**Axis of Evil**" (Jan 2002).
- **Nov 2001** — Bush tells Rumsfeld to draw up Iraq plans; the **Office of Special Plans** (Feith/Wurmser) manufactures intel; **Chalabi files** + **Curveball** (the mobile-labs fabricator) + British "dodgy dossiers."
- **2002** — *NYT*'s **Judith Miller** launders the case ("Black September": aluminum tubes, etc.); Knight Ridder gets it right and is ignored; Phil Donahue fired. The pundit class cheers (Friedman's "suck on this," etc.).
- **Oct 2002** — Congress passes the **Iraq War Resolution** (the 2002 NIE; many never read it).

## The invasion (2002–2003) — *E4*
- **Fall 2002–Feb 2003** — UN dance: **Resolution 1441**; Tenet's "slam dunk"; Blix finds no WMD; **Powell's Feb 5, 2003 UN speech**; **Feb 15** the largest protest in history; **Katharine Gun**'s leak; the second resolution dies.
- **March 19, 2003** — Invasion begins (Dora Farms strike); **March 21** "shock and awe" (~6,000 killed); fierce **Fedayeen** resistance; Chemical Ali airstrike misses.
- **April 9, 2003** — Baghdad falls; the **staged statue toppling**. **May 1** — Bush's "**Mission Accomplished**." The **Jessica Lynch** myth.

## Year Zero — the occupation (2003–2004) — *E6*
- **May 2003** — Bremer arrives; the **CPA**; economic shock therapy; the "Neocon Children's Brigade."
- **CPA Order 1 — de-Ba'athification** and **Order 2 — disbanding the army** (~500k men) seed the insurgency.
- Contractor grift (SAIC, Custer Battles); Chalabi returns with the "Free Iraqi Forces"; **Bernard Kerik** as interior minister.
- **Dec 13, 2003** — Saddam captured (Gaddafi "hands over" WMD days earlier). Attacks climb anyway; Bush forbids the word "insurgency."

## Resistance (2003–2004) — *E7*
- **April 10, 2003 →** the rise of **Muqtada al-Sadr** and the **Mahdi Army** (Sadr City).
- **March 2004** — Bremer shuts Sadr's paper → Mahdi uprising; **Najaf**, **Black Sunday**.
- The **Zarqawi PSYOP** (a manufactured villain; the smoking-gun Pentagon slides).
- **Fallujah** — March 2004 Blackwater killings → the failed April assault → the leveling Nov 2004 second battle (white phosphorus, ~6,000 dead, post-battle police state).
- **April 2004** — **Abu Ghraib** breaks (60 Minutes + Hersh); McChrystal later: it "jolted" nearly every first-time jihadist. Light punishments; Rumsfeld keeps his job.

## Electioneering — two election years (2004–2005) — *E8*
- **Nov 2004** — Bush beats the pro-war Kerry (Swift Boat smears; threat-level games).
- **Jan 30, 2005** — first Iraqi election: Shia bloc 48%, Kurds 26%, the US's Allawi 14%; **Jaafari** PM; Sunni boycott.
- Rice (now SoS) vs. Rumsfeld over "**clear, hold, build**"; Cheney's "last throes."
- **Oct 2005** constitution passed (Shia yes, Sunni no); **Dec 15, 2005** — Chalabi gets <1%; **Nouri al-Maliki** becomes PM (deal brokered by **Soleimani**).

## Civil war & the surge (2006–2008) — *E9, E10*
- **Feb 22, 2006** — the **Samarra** shrine bombing tips Iraq into full **civil war** (Rumsfeld/Casey deny it).
- **Dec 30, 2006** — Saddam's botched, viral **execution** (on Eid) makes him a Sunni martyr.
- **Nov 2006** — Democrats sweep; **Rumsfeld out** (→ Gates).
- **Jan 10, 2007** — the **Surge** (20k+ troops) under **Petraeus**; the **Anbar Awakening** / "Sons of Iraq" + **bribes** to Sunni sheikhs. Violence drops (but mostly due to completed ethnic cleansing + Sadr's ceasefire + tribal deals).
- **Sept 16, 2007** — Blackwater's **Nisour Square** massacre (14 dead; no accountability until 2019).
- **Mid-2008** — the **SOFA** sets full US withdrawal by **Dec 2011**. **Dec 2008** — the **shoe-throwing** at Bush.

## Withdrawal, ISIS, and after (2009–2020) — *E10*
- **Jan 2009** — Obama inaugurated; keeps the withdrawal timeline (last troops out **Dec 2011**); no prosecutions; the war goes quieter (drones, special forces).
- **2011 →** Maliki consolidates power and crushes Arab-Spring protests; mass deprivation persists.
- **2013–2014** — **ISIS** (ex-AQI + ex-Ba'athists, forged in **Camp Bucca**) takes Fallujah/Ramadi, then **Mosul** (summer 2014) → Obama sends troops **back**.
- **Total death toll: ~600,000+** (invasion onward).
- **Jan 2020** — Trump kills **Soleimani** in Iraq; mass protests; Iran the open counterweight.
- **The thesis:** "blowback" isn't karma — it's a **feedback loop**, "the algorithm of empire," and its architects never faced consequences.
