# Blowback S1E3 — "Curveball"

> 📄 **[Read the full transcript](transcripts/s1e03-curveball.md)**


> **Hosts:** Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · **Runtime:** 1:01:03
> **Thesis:** How, after 9/11, the Bush administration pivoted from bin Laden/Afghanistan to **Saddam/Iraq/WMD** — and built a case for war out of recycled intel, fabricators, and one defector codenamed *Curveball*. The hosts assemble the case for war, then take it apart piece by piece.
>
> _Narration order; `[hh:mm:ss]` = audio jump point; real dates in **bold**._

## Cold open: pointing at Saddam on day one `[00:00:01]`
- **Sept 12, 2001** — ex-CIA director **James Woolsey** on CNN: bin Laden "may well have been involved," but "maybe we're supposed to focus solely on bin Laden" — the real "strong suspect" being **the government of Iraq.** He made the rounds of every network that day. (Note for later: Woolsey is a huge backer of Ahmed Chalabi.) `[00:00:01]`

## The 1990s setup: Taliban, bin Laden, and a "dress rehearsal" `[00:03:46]`
- Through the 90s the US had **decent relations with the Taliban** — unsurprising, since the US helped create the anti-Soviet mujahideen — and eyed Afghan/Caspian **oil pipelines**. As late as 1998 the Taliban talked the language of privatization and foreign investment. The "fly in the soup": **Osama bin Laden**, then just one of many wealthy terror financiers, whom Saudi Arabia wanted back. `[00:03:46]`
- **Early 1998** — the Taliban were negotiating with Saudi Arabia and the US to **hand over bin Laden.** Then **Aug 1998** — bin Laden-linked militants bomb the US embassies in **Kenya and Tanzania**. Instead of pressing the handover, the US launched **Operation Infinite Reach**: cruise missiles into Afghanistan and the bombing of the **Al-Shifa pharmaceutical plant in Sudan** (falsely claimed to make nerve gas for bin Laden). It ended Taliban cooperation and pushed them closer to bin Laden. The Sudan strike — attacked for a 9/11-style grievance, hitting a country with nothing to do with it — was "one big **dress rehearsal**." `[00:04:46]`

## 9/11 as opportunity `[00:07:12]`
- For most, 9/11 was "a snuff film broadcast live"; for the **Vulcans** (Cheney, Rumsfeld, Wolfowitz, Rice) it was a chance to cure "Vietnam syndrome," restore moral clarity, and get an agenda. Bush — who in **2000** had campaigned *against* the US being "world cop" — was reborn with a purpose, approval north of **90%**. Beneath the ideology: "a new chance to plunder… new markets." `[00:07:12]`
- **The national psychosis** `[00:10:28]`: civil liberties out the window; FBI "sleeper-cell" hunts; the **AUMF** (the forever-war authorization); the **Oct 2001** Afghanistan war; the creation of **DHS** (parent of ICE). Jonathan Alter's **Nov 4, 2001** Newsweek piece "**Time to Think About Torture**" (floating "transferring suspects to our less squeamish allies") marked a new shamelessness. **Operation TIPS** proposed recruiting 1 in 24 Americans as citizen informants (shelved — the NSA did it better). The **Millennium Challenge 2002** wargame was rigged so the US "blue team" *couldn't lose* (the red general was refused chemical weapons, ordered to pull back and reveal positions) — foreshadowing a hubristic empire that wouldn't consider losing. `[00:10:28]`

## The al-Qaeda myth `[00:15:36]`
- Per scholar **Jason Burke** (*Al-Qaeda*): the idea of a coherent, top-down global network with sleeper cells "simply does not exist." Bin Laden had a real terror group (embassy bombings, the **USS Cole**, 2000) but wasn't calling it "al-Qaeda" — the US slapped that name on it while prosecuting his associates in 2001. He was a **financier "like a venture capitalist,"** not a mastermind, who **puffed himself up** for cameras (hiring armed extras for a CNN interview). The US and bin Laden effectively **co-produced** his bond-villain image (the elaborate cave "fortress" graphic). Countries worldwide then claimed an "al-Qaeda problem" to get US aid — often just relabeling leftists or regime opponents. **No one benefited more from the concept than Saddam's American enemies.** `[00:15:36]`

## Connecting Iraq to 9/11 `[00:20:06]`
- Beyond Woolsey, an outside network (journalists, AEI figures) rushed to link 9/11 to Iraq. Inside the administration, **Rumsfeld** floated striking Iraq first because it "had the best targets" — though for ~a month the focus stayed on Afghanistan, with even **Cheney** telling Wolfowitz to stop talking about Iraq. Rumsfeld's people launched "**Bletchley Park 2**" (a pretentiously named brain-trust w/ Wolfowitz et al.); Woodward described the neocons "rubbing their hands over the idea of war." Their internal logic: Egypt/Saudi (where the hijackers were from) were "intractable," so **"start with Iraq"** — weaker, more vulnerable — plus mention of "Iranian overthrow." `[00:20:06]`
- **Nov 2001** — Bush tells Rumsfeld to draw up Iraq plans. Rumsfeld goes to Gen. **Tommy Franks** (CENTCOM); the inherited Clinton-era plan looked like Gulf War II (half-million troops), so Rumsfeld pushed his "**light footprint**" doctrine. `[00:24:24]`
- **The rhetorical shift** `[00:25:53]`: as Afghanistan wound down (per James Mann's *Rise of the Vulcans*), the message drifted from "al-Qaeda/Osama" to "Saddam/WMD." Cheney on *Meet the Press* (no evidence linking Saddam to 9/11, he admitted) pivoted to the danger of terrorists getting WMD. **Jan 2002 State of the Union** — the "**Axis of Evil**" (Iran, Iraq, North Korea — the last added so it wasn't all-Middle-Eastern; the "shared ideology" was incoherent). `[00:25:53]`

## Manufacturing the intel `[00:29:24]`
- The CIA set up in **Kurdistan** (echo of 1995) — this time **"poison-hunting"** for WMD intel, approached mostly by "alcoholics and people fucking with them" (one man spilled his "deadly poison" on himself); Turkish fixers watched porn and spied on the Americans. `[00:29:24]`
- **The Office of Special Plans** `[00:33:01]` — run by Rumsfeld's undersecretary **Douglas Feith** (openly mocked as stupid even inside the administration), tasked with marshalling intel that Saddam had WMD *and* al-Qaeda ties. His subordinate **David Wurmser** built the "terror connection" by sifting raw intel and drawing conspiracy-board connections (the *It's Always Sunny* blackboard). Per James Risen, **Israeli intelligence** helped convince Wolfowitz to distrust the CIA, pushing him toward Chalabi; OSP analyst **Larry Franklin** was later convicted of **spying for Israel via AIPAC**. `[00:33:01]`
- **Chalabi's role** `[00:36:41]` — already planting Saddam-WMD stories before 9/11 (Feb 2001, Sunday Telegraph); his INC was being audited for fraud (fake invoices to the State Dept) even as he met with Cheney. His intel was funneled to US agencies by **Woolsey** (the day-after-9/11 guy). `[00:36:41]`

## The case for war — and its demolition `[00:38:55]`
- **The list** `[00:38:55]`: chemical/bio **stockpiles**; **aluminum tubes** (alleged centrifuges); **mobile WMD labs**; the post-9/11 **anthrax** letters; **drones** (+ Saddam allegedly buying electronic maps of the **eastern US**); the **Niger yellowcake** uranium claim; **Mohammed Atta's Prague meeting** with Iraqi intel; **al-Qaeda training camps**; and **Zarqawi** ("the new bin Laden").
- **The Chalabi files** `[00:40:13]` (much of the freshest "intel," all bogus — and per biographer Roston, Chalabi *knew* it was false):
  - **Salman Pak** — a Saddam "hijacking sleepaway camp" (3 sources; WaPo Oct 2001, then NYT) `[00:40:50]`
  - an "Iraqi subcontractor" who'd supposedly visited dozens of WMD sites/underground tunnels `[00:41:25]`
  - two 9/11 hijackers meeting Iraqi intel in the **UAE**; the self-proclaimed **inventor of the mobile labs** `[00:42:16]`
  - **Saddam's "mistress"** — a Greek woman (Reuters!) describing Viagra/"Heil Hitler" rants and Saddam meeting bin Laden — even the INC told her to tone it down; she recanted `[00:42:44]`
  - a **resurrected dead Navy pilot** — KIA in 1991, whom Chalabi claimed was alive as a POW `[00:43:49]`
  - **Chalabi's tell** `[00:45:03]`: months after the invasion, asked by Barton Gellman what happens if no WMD are found, he says *"turn off the tape… It doesn't matter. We liberated Iraq, and it does not matter why."*
- **Curveball** `[00:45:55]` — the episode's namesake: an Iraqi defector who showed up in **Germany in 1999** seeking a visa (an alleged sex offender, low-level engineer), the key source for **mobile WMD labs.** German intelligence (the only ones to debrief him) called his info "highly suspect"; US agents never debriefed him — yet in 2002 the DIA/CIA passed his raw claims to top officials and Congress.
- **The British "dodgy dossiers"** (Blair) `[00:47:55]`: the **Niger uranium** claim (forged documents); the "**45 minutes**" claim (actually about conventional battlefield weapons; Tenet privately: "the 'they can attack in 45 minutes' shit"); a **green-vials-of-VX** report lifted from the movie *The Rock*; and a section **plagiarized from a Cal State student's paper** (parodied in *In the Loop*).
- **Rapid-fire debunk** `[00:50:00]`: aluminum tubes = cheap rockets; Prague meeting = didn't happen (Czech intel said so); anthrax = made up; drones = surveillance; eastern-US map = never bought. *"It never happened… it's an urban legend that never happened."*

## Selling it: the public, the Congress `[00:50:21]`
- **The anti-war movement** swelled through fall 2002 — by late October, ~**100,000** in DC and **50,000** in SF; even bigger in Europe (UK ~150k in Sept, **1 million in Italy** in Nov) — peaking in early 2003. But "the US Congress doesn't run on protests." `[00:50:21]`
- **The Congressional push** `[00:52:32]`: the **Iraq War Resolution** introduced **Oct 2, 2002**; Bush's **Oct 7 Cincinnati** speech ("we cannot wait… the smoking gun that could come in the form of a mushroom cloud"); privately Bush said he'd "take over the oil fields." The **2002 National Intelligence Estimate** packaged all the junk as fact — and many senators (some future presidential candidates) admitted they **never read it**. A Rose Garden event paired **John McCain** and **Joe Lieberman** (both 2000 Bush opponents) in support. `[00:52:32]`
- **The vote (mid-Oct 2002)** `[00:57:49]` — dissenters: **Barbara Lee**, **Bernie Sanders** (warning of unintended consequences and civil war), **Jim McDermott**. A closing montage of future leaders voting **yes**: **Hillary Clinton**, **Joe Biden** ("a march to peace and security"), **John Kerry**, **John McCain** — and (the clip notes) the *opposite* direction from people like Mike Pence. `[00:57:49]`
- **Cliffhanger:** next episode → the UN, and the anti-war movement at its peak. `[00:51:54]`
