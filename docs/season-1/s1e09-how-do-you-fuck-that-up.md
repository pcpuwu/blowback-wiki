## Where we are `[00:01:17]`
- **Dec 2005** — Maliki is PM; Bush's approval slides into the 30s for the rest of his term; the country careens toward civil war, and the only "answer" is the one Rumsfeld won't allow: more troops — the **Surge**. But first, two reckonings: Saddam, and Rumsfeld. `[00:01:17]`

## The real Saddam `[00:03:05]`
- After his **Dec 2003** capture, CIA debriefer **John Nixon** spent hours with Saddam (book, 2016) and found a man utterly unlike the Hitlerian image: he wasn't running Iraq day-to-day, was **bored with politics** (working on his **novel**), and was obsessed mostly with **money** ("Do you think Saddam is a tradesman?"). He'd even assumed **9/11 would bring the US closer** — as in the 80s, when Washington funded him against radical Islamists (Iran). The men delivering his fate: a Bush (whose father worked with Saddam) and **Rumsfeld** (who'd flown to Baghdad in the 80s to offer Reagan's support). `[00:03:05]`

## The trial and execution `[00:09:01]`
- **July 2004** — proceedings begin before an **Iraqi Special Tribunal** set up by Bremer at Rumsfeld's direction (led by **Salem Chalabi**, Ahmed's nephew). Rumsfeld **denied Saddam POW status** — a deliberate "fuck you" to the International Criminal Court (no Hague, unlike Milošević). Of all his crimes (gassing the Kurds — US-approved; draining the marshes), he was charged with the **Dujail** massacre (~148 Shia killed, 1982). Convicted **Nov 2006**. `[00:09:01]`
- **Dec 30, 2006 — the execution** `[00:11:36]`: hanged on the holiday of **Eid**, at a base called **Camp Justice**, with Sadrist guards taunting him — all captured on a **cell-phone video** that went viral and made Saddam a **martyr** for Sunnis (even reported copycat hangings). The US disclaimed responsibility ("that's an Iraqi government decision") despite having set up the whole apparatus. A galvanizing moment for Sunni militancy. `[00:11:36]`

## Civil war, 2006 `[00:15:11]`
- Iraq was a failed state — roving gangs, ~30 kidnappings/month, ~4 Iraqis/week killed by US troops, electricity *still* below pre-war levels, unemployment 25–40%, rampant police corruption. Iraqi voice **Ra'ed**'s family: he was kidnapped by a militia and fled; his mother carjacked; his brother abducted by an **Interior Ministry** militia (his father searched morgues for two weeks before a ransom freed him) — a microcosm of millions of stories. `[00:15:11]`
- **Feb 22, 2006 — the Samarra bombing** `[00:19:32]`: the destruction of the **al-Askari "Golden Dome" shrine** (one of Shia Islam's holiest) tipped ad-hoc violence into **systematic ethnic cleansing**. The US blamed al-Qaeda/Zarqawi; the Shia reprisals were real — but **Rumsfeld and Gen. George Casey denied/downplayed** them, blaming "exaggerated" media reports for Americans believing there was a civil war. `[00:19:32]`

## Rumsfeld falls `[00:23:16]`
- Post-2004, **Rice → Secretary of State** (Hadley as NSA). Rice admitted on *Meet the Press* there was **"no plan B."** `[00:23:16]`
- **Rumsfeld's arc** `[00:25:29]`: once a celebrity (a hagiographic Midge Decter book; *People*'s "sexiest men alive"; the witty "Rummy show" briefings), his bluster curdled as Iraq collapsed — he was yelled at by military families in Alaska for lying (on tape). Congress commissioned the bipartisan **Iraq Study Group** (Baker–Hamilton), which found brutal political stasis: Maliki (a sectarian Shia) was using the "Ba'athist" label to purge more Sunnis — the government *reifying* Shia dominance rather than sharing power. `[00:25:29]`
- **Nov 2006 midterms** `[00:32:56]` — Democrats swept House and Senate. Rumsfeld — who'd survived the invasion, Bremer, the insurgency lies, Guantanamo, and *Abu Ghraib* — finally got the axe, replaced by **Robert Gates**. A week later Bush ordered the strategic review that became the Surge. `[00:32:56]`

## Petraeus & the Surge `[00:34:57]`
- **David Petraeus** — the legend (shot through the chest in a 1991 training accident, didn't flinch), the "brainiac soldier-scholar" the media went gaga for ("Can this man save Iraq?"). He became the public face of the Surge — Bush name-checked him 150+ times in six months. `[00:34:57]`
- **Jan 10, 2007** — Bush commits **20,000+ additional troops** (mostly to Baghdad) under the banner of **"counterinsurgency."** `[00:37:27]`
- **What actually happened** `[00:42:51]`: the **Anbar Awakening** — Sunni tribes turned on the now-unpopular al-Qaeda/Zarqawi (Zarqawi killed June 2006, which "didn't matter") and were folded into the **"Sons of Iraq"** (basically **re-Ba'athification**). The hidden engine was **bribes** — ~$150M to Sunni sheikhs, who skimmed ~20% per fighter and got rich, **consolidating warlord power**. Per CFR's Steve Simon, this was outsourced security that set Iraq up to become **ungovernable** (foreshadowing **ISIS**). `[00:42:51]`
- **The myth** `[00:46:43]`: violence did drop — but mostly because the **ethnic cleansing was already complete**, al-Sadr had declared a **ceasefire**, and the Sunni tribal deals held — *not* because more troops "won." Crediting the Surge was "a tremendous accomplishment of military propaganda." `[00:46:43]`

## Blackwater & the privatized war `[00:50:31]`
- Rumsfeld arrived at the Pentagon in 2001 to *cut* its (un-auditable, trillions-missing) budget — his solution after 9/11 was to **outsource and privatize**: a "shadow army" of ~100,000 contractors alongside 150,000 troops. PMCs did everything from cafeteria food to pulling triggers; Halliburton (ex-CEO: **Cheney**) got the no-bid mega-contract; **Blackwater** (founder **Eric Prince**, Betsy DeVos's brother) guarded Bremer ($21M). `[00:50:31]`
- **Nisour Square (Sept 16, 2007)** `[00:55:01]`: at the very moment the US was bragging about the Surge, Blackwater guards opened fire in a Baghdad square, killing **14 unarmed civilians** (17 wounded). The Iraqis couldn't prosecute; US indictments were tossed; only in **2019** did the guard who fired first get a life sentence. PMCs were also a way to wage a Vietnam-scale war **without a draft** — and they'd outlast the troops. (Prince later rebranded Blackwater repeatedly — Xe, Academi — and pitched privatized war in Afghanistan/China.) `[00:55:01]`
- **Cliffhanger:** next (final) episode → the withdrawal, and ISIS. `[00:59:55]`
