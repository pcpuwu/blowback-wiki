# Blowback Season 5 — Master Timeline (Cambodia)

> A single **real-world chronological spine** across all 10 episodes. Each per-episode note (`s5eNN-*.md`) walks its episode in *narration* order; this file reorders by date. `(E#)` = the episode(s) that cover it. Sources: [`s5-sources.md`](s5-sources.md).
>
> _(NB: Blowback Season 5 is **Cambodia**, not Iran — the season was mislabeled in earlier project notes.)_

## Ancient Indochina & French colonialism (pre-1945) — *E2*
- The **Khmer Empire** (Angkor) dominates Southeast Asia for half a millennium, then declines, picked apart by Vietnam and Thailand.
- **1850s–1890s** — France colonizes Indochina, "dismembering" it (Vietnam the crown jewel; Cambodia and Laos as buffers). Importing Vietnamese labor/bureaucrats into Cambodia breeds lasting resentment; delayed Khmer education stunts Cambodian nationalism.
- **1930** — Ho Chi Minh founds the **Indochinese Communist Party**. **1940** — the Khmer Issarak independence movement.

## WWII → the First Indochina War (1945–54) — *E2*
- **1941** — France's Vichy regime crowns the "pliable" young **Sihanouk**; Japan takes Indochina.
- **Sept 2, 1945** — Ho declares Vietnam's independence; **Dec 1946** — the First Indochina War begins.
- **Saloth Sâr** (the future Pol Pot) studies in Paris (1949), joins Marxist circles.
- **May 7, 1954** — **Dien Bien Phu** (Giap defeats France); the **Geneva Conference** splits Vietnam at the 17th parallel, frees Sihanouk's Cambodia, and *betrays the Cambodian communists* (unseated, denied territory — the Khmer Rouge's founding grievance).

## The American war & Cambodia's tightrope (1955–68) — *E3*
- **1955** — Sihanouk abdicates to rule as a politician (the Sangkum); pursues Cold War neutrality (refusing SEATO). *(E3)*
- The US builds **Diem**'s South Vietnam against Geneva's reunification elections; the **Sino-Soviet split** begins; the secret war in **Laos**; the Khmer Rouge driven into the jungle.
- **Aug 1964** — the **Gulf of Tonkin** fabrication. **1965** — Rolling Thunder, the troop surge. **Jan 1968** — the **Tet Offensive**; My Lai. **1968** — Nixon & Kissinger **sabotage the Paris peace talks** to win the election. *(E3)*

## Nixon, Kissinger & the destruction of Cambodia (1969–73) — *E4, E5, E6*
- **March 1969** — the secret bombing (**Operation Menu**) begins, with a dual-reporting cover-up. *(E4)*
- **March 18, 1970** — the US-blessed **Lon Nol coup** ousts Sihanouk (who joins FUNK in Beijing); **April 30, 1970** — the **invasion of Cambodia** → **Kent State** and the home-front explosion. Saloth Sâr becomes **Pol Pot**. *(E4)*
- **1970–72** — the China opening (ping-pong diplomacy, Kissinger's secret trip, the 1972 Nixon visit); Lon Nol's republic rots (anti-Vietnamese pogroms); the Khmer Rouge come into their own. *(E5)*
- **Jan 27, 1973** — the **Paris Accords** end America's Vietnam War (≈the 1968 terms Nixon sabotaged). The **bombing of Cambodia escalates** to its peak (81 runs/day; the Neak Luong strike). *(E5, E6)*
- **Aug 15, 1973** — the bombing ends (per a congressional deal) amid Watergate ("the Southeast Asia Division of Watergate"); the Cambodia impeachment article is later abandoned. *(E6)*

## The fall & the Khmer Rouge (1975) — *E6, E7*
- **April 17, 1975** — the **Khmer Rouge take Phnom Penh** (the silent victors); **April 30** — Saigon falls (→ Ho Chi Minh City). *(E6)*
- **"Year Zero"** — the cities emptied (~20,000 die on the marches), the slave society under **Angkar** (no money, markets, religion, family; "new people" vs. "old people"; mass death). The **Mayaguez** incident (the last 41 US dead of the Indochina war). *(E7)*
- **Sept 1976** — Mao dies; a week later Pol Pot announces Angkar is Marxist-Leninist; the official Democratic Kampuchea government. *(E7)*

## The genocide & the road to war (1977–78) — *E7, E8*
- **1977** — failed US-Vietnam normalization (the POW/MIA myth, Nixon's secret reparations letter); the Khmer Rouge reignite the border war with **Vietnam** (Chau Doc). *(E7)*
- **The genocide machine** — **Tuol Sleng (S-21)** under Duch; the self-cannibalizing purges; the extermination of Vietnamese, Chinese, Thai, and **Cham** minorities. **Sept 1977** — Pol Pot reveals himself; the **Ba Chuc** massacre (~3,500). *(E8)*
- **1978** — **Brzezinski** engineers a US-China entente against Soviet-backed Vietnam; the So Phim Eastern Zone revolt and purge; **Nov 3, 1978** — Vietnam signs a friendship treaty with the USSR. *(E8)*

## The Third Indochina War & its aftermath (1979–89) — *E8, E9*
- **Dec 25, 1978** — **Vietnam invades Cambodia**; **Jan 7, 1979** — Phnom Penh falls; the Khmer Rouge experiment ends. *(E8)*
- The US **condemns Vietnam**, blocks aid amid famine; Carter charges the *Vietnamese* with "genocide." **Feb 1979** — **China invades Vietnam** (a punitive war). The **Faustian pact** — the US/China/Thailand back the Khmer Rouge as an insurgency; the Khmer Rouge **keep Cambodia's UN seat**. *(E9)*
- **1979** — the Vietnamese-run **genocide tribunal** convicts Pol Pot in absentia (ignored by the West); Pol Pot, "Phem," dons safari suits. *(E9)*

## The end & the long aftermath (1988–present) — *E10*
- **1988–89** — Vietnam **withdraws** unilaterally; the US finally drops the Khmer Rouge (Baker, 1990). *(E10)*
- **Oct 1991** — the **Paris Peace Accords** + UNTAC; **May 1993** — Cambodia's first election (Khmer Rouge boycott; Hun Sen forces a co-PM deal). *(E10)*
- **1996–97** — Khmer Rouge mass defections; Pol Pot murders Son Sen, is tried by Ta Mok; **April 15, 1998** — **Pol Pot dies**. **July 1997** — Hun Sen's coup → unchallenged rule for 25 years. *(E10)*
- **2003–2022** — the **ECCC** tribunal convicts Duch, Nuon Chea, and Khieu Samphan (genocide of the Vietnamese and Cham) — scope limited to 1975–79, leaving out the US bombing and Lon Nol era.
- **2012** — Sihanouk dies (89). **Today** — Cambodia (run by Hun Sen's son **Hun Manet**) aligns with China (a Chinese naval base replacing a US-built one); the region restructured by US-China rivalry — the latest of **Kissinger's "gray areas."** *(E10)*
