## Cold open: the chain reaction `[00:00:06]`
- A montage of escalation: the **Gulf of Tonkin** (Aug 1964, an attack Johnson privately doubted) → Nixon & Kissinger's **secret bombing of Cambodia** (March 1969) → the **invasion** (spring 1970) → the **Khmer Rouge** takeover (April 1975, forced labor and extermination) → **Vietnam invades Cambodia** to oust Pol Pot (Dec 1978) → the US condemns it and begins "a decade-long policy of support for the Khmer Rouge." "We're still fighting the Vietnam War, and this is the last battle of that war, and if we have to use the Khmer Rouge as a pawn in that, we'll use them." `[00:00:06]`
- Roadmap: "the story of how a small, neutral country… got fed to the meat grinder." Not just Vietnam but "a constellation of conflicts… a chain reaction" through Southeast Asia, China, Thailand, Laos, and especially Cambodia — culminating in Pol Pot's 1975 seizure (the "killing fields"), then the war in which *Vietnam, America's enemy, toppled Pol Pot and stopped the Cambodian genocide.* The senior general's line: "when the wolves are closing in, you throw them something off and let them chew it." `[00:03:27]`

## The themes `[00:07:00]`
- **The crack-up of Asian communism** — "the less popular counterpart to the collapse of the Eastern Bloc." The cast: **Ho Chi Minh**, **Nixon**, **Pol Pot**, **Kissinger**, China's **Zhou Enlai**, and "the most enigmatic character we've ever featured" — Prince **Norodom Sihanouk**. `[00:07:00]`
- **China** `[00:09:08]`: a tale about America's now-most-important relationship — a period when US and Chinese interests "met in an embrace" (Nixon/Kissinger's opening to China, still officially celebrated there) "at the expense of what some called Red Brotherhood in Asia." (The largest Belt and Road project to date is a Cambodian expressway.) `[00:09:08]`
- **Yankees vs. Cowboys** `[00:09:49]`: a sequel to Season 2 (Cuba) via Carl Oglesby's *The Yankee and Cowboy War* — the liberal pro-NATO eastern establishment ("Yankee") vs. the hyper-nationalist frontier tycoons ("Cowboy" — LBJ, Nixon, the Bay of Pigs team). The palace struggle spills out of the Western Hemisphere into Southeast Asia, "not at the ends of communist bayonets, but American bombs." `[00:09:49]`

## The hosts' travels `[00:11:48]`
- **March 2024** — they visited Cambodia and Vietnam (the killing fields, the **Tuol Sleng** torture center, Angkor Wat, towns famous for US bombing; Vietnamese villages near the border that were sites of Khmer Rouge massacres). Residents of **Neak Luong** described unexploded bombs still buried in ponds and decades of landmine casualties; **Mrs. Trúc** in southern Vietnam described a midnight Khmer Rouge massacre of her village; a Tuol Sleng survivor recalled his liberation by Vietnamese forces; a mechanic described the country's enduring poverty. `[00:11:48]`

## The propaganda lineage `[00:16:14]`
- **Dr. Tom Dooley** — the Catholic "humanitarian" poster boy of 1950s–60s Indochina (quoted in *The Exorcist*'s epigraph: communists driving chopsticks into boys' ears), revealed later as a **CIA asset** whose atrocity stories were fabricated propaganda (run by Allen Dulles and Edward Lansdale) — a strategy of "smuggling agency work through religious philanthropy" that began in Korea and spread to Cambodia (a "massive Christian CIA collaboration"). `[00:16:14]`
- **The basket-case regime** `[00:18:53]`: the post-invasion Cambodian government — "a mafia with a state" (no-show jobs, fake invoices, a commander who filled his office with miniature furniture to look bigger, a drunk who had a soldier accidentally blow off part of his head). `[00:18:53]`
- **The POW/MIA myth** `[00:20:37]`: Nixon privately promised Vietnam reparations (a year's GDP) — then, to avoid paying, the administration *invented* the **MIA category** (renaming "killed in action, body unrecovered" as "missing in action / POW"), turning dead soldiers into imagined hostages. Despite Congress debunking it, the POW/MIA flag flies on. `[00:20:37]`

## The buried scandal `[00:23:02]`
- A 1990 NYT op-ed: "Pol Pot: Brutal, Yes, But No Mass Murderer." A *communist* army (Vietnam) drove out the "communist boogeyman" Pol Pot — and was met with US-led condemnation, sanctions, and blocked food aid amid a looming famine. **Carter** invoked "genocide" — not against the Khmer Rouge but against the *Vietnamese* who ousted them. At the UN the US fought to *keep the Khmer Rouge in Cambodia's seat* (a US delegate found Khmer Rouge leader **Ieng Sary** grinning and shaking his hand). `[00:23:02]`

## The main cast `[00:25:11]`
- **Prince Norodom Sihanouk** `[00:25:11]` — "a naughty boy," Cambodia's head of state for most of the 20th century, who switched sides endlessly; coined the term "Khmer Rouge," cracked down on the young radicals (and co-opted them); lifelong friend of Zhou Enlai and Kim Il-sung; made his cabinet act in his homemade movies.
- **Henry Kissinger** `[00:28:07]` — the German-Jewish refugee turned celebrity diplomat and Nixon's "spiritual partner," who prolonged Vietnam, brought in Cambodia, and made peace with China ("disposing of small countries like paper").
- **Lon Nol** `[00:30:38]` — "Black Papa," the Khmer-supremacist devout-Buddhist general (a "fascist scarecrow") whose 1970 coup and anti-Vietnamese pogroms helped wreck Cambodia; in neurological decline from a stroke.
- **Nixon** `[00:33:18]` — the paranoid outsider remapping American power in secret (backing Yahya Khan against Bangladesh, the Allende coup in Chile, the drug war) — his ultimate puzzle Indochina, where failure stoked his rage.
- **Pol Pot** `[00:37:11]` — born **Saloth Sâr** to a prosperous, palace-connected family; a shy, polite boy; a 1949 Paris scholarship → clandestine left-wing politics → the jungle *maquis*, where he took the meaningless name "Pol Pot" ("even his cipher was a cipher").

## The season ahead `[00:39:17]`
- From French colonialism → Cambodian/Vietnamese nationalism → the **1954 Geneva Conference** (Vietnam split; Sihanouk gets Cambodian independence) → the Second Indochina (Vietnam) War of the 1960s → Nixon/Kissinger's secret bombing and 1970 invasion → the **Lon Nol** coup and Cambodian civil war → the **Khmer Rouge** victory (1975, "Democratic Kampuchea," three and a half years of the killing fields) → **Vietnam's 1979 ouster of Pol Pot** → and the US/China backing of the Khmer Rouge against Vietnam and the USSR through the 80s. "The snake eats its tail once again." Guests: Elizabeth Becker, Vuong-Quan Hoang/Nu-Anh Tran [historian], Seymour Hersh, Ben Kiernan. `[00:39:17]`
