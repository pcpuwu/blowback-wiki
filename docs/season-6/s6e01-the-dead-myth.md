> **Thesis:** The season-opener for **Angola** — the Cold War's biggest showdown in Africa. A revolution against Portugal's backward empire, then a civil war in which the socialist **MPLA** (backed by **Cuba** and the USSR) faced the CIA, mercenaries, **apartheid South Africa**, and the ex-Maoist warlord **Jonas Savimbi** (UNITA). Mandela's verdict: Cuba's defeat of the South Africans in Angola "destroyed the myth of the invincibility of the white oppressor" — the turning point against apartheid.

## Cold open: Mandela and the dead myth `[00:00:03]`
- **June 1990** — newly-freed **Nelson Mandela** at City College got hostile questions (Ken Adelman) over praising Castro, Gaddafi, and Arafat. Mandela: "the mistake… is to think that their enemies should be our enemy. Our attitude towards any country is determined by the attitude of that country to our struggle." He embraced **Castro** for life — because **Cuba**, via tens of thousands of troops in **Angola**, defeated apartheid South Africa "again and again," which "destroyed the myth of the invincibility of the white oppressor… the turning point for the liberation of our continent." `[00:00:03]`

## The season `[00:08:00]`
- The Cold War showdown in Africa: a revolution against Portugal (the dictator **Salazar**), then a scramble after the empire's mid-70s collapse won by the socialist **MPLA** — unwelcome to its rivals, the CIA, and South Africa (which wanted to spread apartheid across southern Africa). In **1975** the MPLA's rivals + mercenaries + South Africa invaded — and Cuba answered the call from thousands of miles away. The show's first Africa season; a "constellation of conflicts" (like the Cambodia season). `[00:08:00]`
- **The neighborhood** `[00:10:25]`: **Namibia** (a South-Africa-occupied former German colony, with the **SWAPO** resistance — much of the action driven by Namibia's status, the MPLA aiding SWAPO); **Zaire** (the US-allied dictator **Mobutu**, who'd disposed of Lumumba); cross-border separatist groups. A small Caribbean nation "poking the American giant" not in Latin America but halfway across the world — "a terrible story, without a true, happy ending" (hundreds of thousands dead, limbs blown off, famine). `[00:10:25]`

## The mercenaries `[00:13:36]`
- The MPLA's enemies called the *Cubans* the mercenaries — untrue (Cuba lost money on the mission). The real mercenaries were American/European glory-seekers ("unemployed bricklayers… marital misfits"; one, Gary Acker, jailed in Angola). Plus serious European/African professional soldiers who fought both sides. `[00:13:36]`

## The nuclear dimension `[00:16:35]`
- **South Africa** (cornered after Angola and Mozambique went socialist in 1974) accelerated its **nuclear program** — at least six atomic bombs. Castro had Cuban troops travel at night, split up, "always keeping in mind the possibility that the enemy might use nuclear weapons." South Africa's partner in building the bomb was **Israel** ("the link was very intimate" — a 1979 Indian Ocean flash interpreted as a joint test). `[00:16:35]`

## Red Scorpion & the culture war `[00:19:01]`
- **1988** — *Red Scorpion* (Dolph Lundgren), explicitly meant to build up **Jonas Savimbi**'s image and serve as a stand-in for the anti-Soviet Afghan war — financed and supported by **apartheid South Africa** (shot in Namibia with SADF tanks/troops; light-skinned Namibians as "Cuban soldiers"). The producer: **Jack Abramoff** (Savimbi's PR man, later a convicted fraudster). It became a lightning rod for anti-apartheid activism (Warner Bros. pulled out). `[00:19:01]`

## The cast `[00:23:33]`
- **Jonas Savimbi** (UNITA) — Baptist-raised, polyglot, ambitious; zigzagged from Maoism to "moderate" to a Reagan-era anti-communist icon (Stinger missiles, White House guest), based in the southern/central countryside, backed by South Africa — later exposed for child soldiers, witch-burning, sexual abuse, ivory/diamond trafficking, and blowing up peace talks. Diplomat Chaz Freeman: "he had cerebral malaria, which made him murderous" (Freeman bringing him books "as an insurance policy against being strangled"). `[00:23:33]`
- **Cuba** `[00:28:04]`: Castro's Cuba sent doctors and aid worldwide and backed African anti-colonial movements (Che in the Congo, Algeria, Guinea-Bissau). **Autumn 1975** — facing the MPLA's likely fall, Cuba decided to intervene despite Soviet and US disapproval — "the great Cuban idealism" (Gleijeses): "in terms of realpolitik it was in Cuba's interest to do nothing, but Fidel decides to send the troops." The first ~90 special forces stopped the South African advance. `[00:28:04]`
- **Reagan** `[00:33:33]`: a "right-wing extremist" who took Carter's policy and built "a much larger Frankenstein" — the **Contra** scheme repeated worldwide (Nicaragua, Afghanistan, Cambodia, and now Angola's Savimbi). William Minter's book title: *Apartheid's Contras*. `[00:33:33]`
- **Agostinho Neto** `[00:37:05]` — "healer, soldier, and poet" (Achebe), the preeminent MPLA leader (imprisoned by Portugal); ruled ruthlessly amid coup attempts, but for Castro "the man to back" against the CIA-backed **Holden Roberto** (FNLA) and the South-Africa-backed Savimbi.
- **Salazar** `[00:43:00]` — the austere economist-dictator of Portugal for 40+ years; a reactionary nationalist who kept his distance from fascism, despised democracy, ran a secret police, demanded the colonies (Angola, Mozambique, Guinea-Bissau, Goa), and used a NATO airbase as a bargaining chip against the US's anti-colonial complex.

## The season ahead `[00:42:59]`
- From the Portuguese slave/coffee colony → 1960s Angolan nationalism → the early-70s three-faction struggle (MPLA, FNLA, UNITA) → the **Carnation Revolution** ending Portugal's empire → South Africa's 1975 invasion denied by the 11th-hour Cuban commitment → a two-decade **civil war** (Savimbi backed by South Africa, the US, and the Safari Club) → the legendary battle of **Cuito Cuanavale** (late 80s) → Savimbi's death (2002) and an uneasy peace. Guests: Antonio Tomás, Piero Gleijeses, Marissa Moorman, William Minter, Chaz Freeman, Justin Pearce. `[00:42:59]`
