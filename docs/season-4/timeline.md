# Blowback Season 4 — Master Timeline (Afghanistan)

> A single **real-world chronological spine** across all 10 episodes. Each per-episode note (`s4eNN-*.md`) walks its episode in *narration* order; this file reorders by date. `(E#)` = the episode(s) that cover it. Sources: [`s4-sources.md`](s4-sources.md).

## Old Afghanistan & the British (10th c.–1947) — *E2*
- **10th–18th c.** — the crossroads of empires; **Ahmad Shah Durrani** founds the modern Afghan empire (1747).
- **1839 / 1878 / 1919** — the three **Anglo-Afghan Wars**; the **Durand Line** drawn (1890s); Britain backs religious revolts against modernizing kings (Amanullah's reforms reversed, late 1920s).
- **1947** — Britain's partition of India creates **Pakistan**; Afghanistan votes against its UN admission.

## The Cold War prelude (1950s–1978) — *E2*
- **1950s** — PM **Daoud** modernizes with Soviet aid ("light my American cigarette with a Russian match"); the Afghan Communist Party forms.
- **1950s–60s** — the CIA cultivates Afghans (the **Hafizullah Amin**/Columbia question); Soviet aid "immensely successful."
- **1973** — Daoud's coup (a republic); Pakistan/ISI begins training **Hekmatyar** and **Massoud**; the **Safari Club** (post-Watergate outsourced CIA dirty work, **BCCI**) targets Afghanistan.
- **April 1978** — the **Saur Revolution** (the Khalq communists kill Daoud; Taraki president).

## The road to invasion (1978–79) — *E2*
- **1978–79** — the revolution unravels (Stalinist terror, mullah-led revolt); the US "**bleeders**" (Brzezinski) sideline the "dealers." **March 1979** — the Herat uprising; the Soviets *repeatedly refuse* troops.
- **Sept 1979** — **Amin** kills Taraki. **Dec 1979** — after Iran's revolution and the Mecca/embassy attacks, the Politburo decides; **Christmas Eve, 1979 — 80,000 Soviet troops invade** (the Brzezinski "Afghan trap").

## The Soviet war (1979–89) — *E3, E4*
- **Dec 27, 1979** — Soviets storm Amin's palace; **Babrak Karmal** installed. *(E3)*
- **1980** — Carter's reprisals (grain embargo, Olympic boycott, the **Carter Doctrine**); the propaganda war ("Russia's Vietnam," Dan Rather's "Gunga Dan," fabricated obituaries, "yellow rain" WMD smears). *(E3)*
- **1982–85** — Avrakotos's "dirty dozen," **Charlie Wilson**, **Joanne Herring**, and Pakistan's **Zia**; heroin via BCCI; **Vickers**'s "techno-guerrillas"; **Operation Cyclone** balloons to billions; cross-border raids into the USSR; the **Afghan-Arabs** (bin Laden, Azzam, Al-Kifah). *(E3, E4)*
- **March 1985** — **Gorbachev** takes over, calls the war "a bleeding wound" (Feb 1986), decides to withdraw. *(E4)*
- **Sept 1986** — the **Stinger** missile arrives (Engineer Ghaffar's first kills). *(E4)*
- **April 14, 1988** — the **Geneva Accords** (US refuses to stop arming the rebels; the mujahideen refuse to sign); **Zia killed** in an Aug 1988 plane crash. **Feb 15, 1989** — the last Soviet troops cross Friendship Bridge. *(E4)*

## The interregnum & civil war (1989–96) — *E5*
- **1989** — Najibullah's government survives the warlord assault on **Jalalabad**; bin Laden returns to Saudi Arabia.
- **1991–92** — the **USSR collapses**; Soviet aid ends; **Najibullah falls** (April 1992), arrested by Dostum.
- **1992–96** — the **battle for Kabul** (Massoud vs. Hekmatyar) shreds the city (the "small Holocaust," ~65,000 dead); blowback on US soil (the Kahane murder; the **1993 WTC bombing**); bin Laden in Sudan declares war (1996).

## The Taliban (1994–2001) — *E5, E6*
- **1994** — **Mullah Omar** and the **Taliban** rise from Kandahar (Pakistani/ISI-backed). **Sept 26, 1996** — they take **Kabul** and murder Najibullah; the **Islamic Emirate** (75% of world heroin; the **Unocal** pipeline courtship; "we can live with that"). *(E5)*
- **1996–2001** — the jihad goes global (Bosnia, the Philippines, Algeria); the embassy bombings (**Aug 7, 1998**); the **Mazar-i-Sharif** massacre; the **USS Cole** (Oct 2000); the spiked intelligence on the hijackers (al-Mihdhar/al-Hazmi, the Saudi handler al-Bayoumi); pipeline diplomacy to the eve of the attacks; **Massoud assassinated Sept 9, 2001**. *(E6)*

## The US invasion (2001) — *E6, E7*
- **Sept 11, 2001** — the attacks; Rumsfeld's "Go massive." **Oct 7** — Operation Enduring Freedom's air war; the AUMF, the Patriot Act, assassination/rendition authority, Cheney's COG. *(E7)*
- **Nov 2001** — the Taliban fall, won by **bribery** (MI6/CIA) more than bombs; **Dasht-i-Leili** container massacre (Dostum); the **Kunduz airlift** (Cheney-approved). **Dec 2001** — **Karzai** installed at Bonn; **bin Laden escapes Tora Bora**. *(E7)*

## The Bush occupation (2002–08) — *E8*
- **Jan 2002** — the "**Axis of Evil**" turn against Iran. The warlords (drug rackets, bacha bazi) restored; cash to Pakistan, Uzbekistan, and the Karzai family; **black sites** and torture (Dilawar's death at Bagram); **Daniel Pearl** beheaded (2002).
- **Dec 2003 Loya Jirga** — **Malalai Joya**'s defiant speech. The **contractor** bonanza (Halliburton/KBR); heroin hits **92%** of global supply (mostly US-allied hands).
- **Oct 2004** — Karzai elected; bin Laden's tape helps re-elect Bush. **2006–08** — the Taliban comeback (IEDs, suicide bombing); A.Q. Khan pardoned; **Benazir Bhutto assassinated** (Dec 2007); a suicide attack targets Cheney at Bagram (2007).

## The Obama war (2009–16) — *E9*
- **2009** — the surge; McKiernan fired; **McChrystal** and **COIN**; Obama sends 30,000 with an 18-month deadline; **Karzai's stolen election** (1M fraudulent votes).
- **2010** — Kabul Bank's $1B fraud; "Money as a Weapons System"; the failed **Marja** operation; McChrystal flamed out by Rolling Stone → **Petraeus**.
- **May 1, 2011** — **bin Laden killed** at Abbottabad (the ISI's hidden "desk"; Hersh's walk-in account). **Jan 2011** — the **Raymond Davis** affair turns Pakistan against the US.
- **Dec 2014** — Obama declares "end of combat" (Resolute Support) — but the war continues; **Ashraf Ghani** replaces Karzai (2014); a US gunship hits a **Doctors Without Borders hospital** in Kunduz (2015); **ISIS** expands into Afghanistan.

## The end & aftermath (2016–2023) — *E10*
- **2017** — Hekmatyar's return; the **MOAB**; civilian airstrike deaths up ~330%. Mullah Omar revealed dead (2013); **Hibatullah** leads the Taliban.
- **Feb 29, 2020** — the **US–Taliban Doha deal** (government excluded). **April–Aug 2021** — Biden's withdrawal; **Kabul falls** (Aug 15); Ghani flees; the chaotic evacuation (~200,000 allies left behind; 13 US troops killed).
- **2022–23** — Zawahiri killed in Kabul (2022); frozen Afghan assets → mass famine (43% on <1 meal/day); the snake eats its tail (Syria, and Afghan Mi-17s sent to Ukraine to kill Russians). ~243,000 dead in the Afghanistan-Pakistan war zone since 2001.
