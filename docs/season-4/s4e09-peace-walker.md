## "Finish the job" `[00:00:24]`
- **2008** — candidate Obama promised to end Iraq and *escalate* Afghanistan ("the central front… is not Iraq, and it never was"). The carousel of generals (McKiernan, Petraeus, McChrystal) and the Clinton–Holbrooke team would sell "the mirage of the good war." Whitlock: Obama staffers took the statistical deception "to a new level" — "an eight-year campaign of denial, fantasia, and sometimes outright lies." `[00:00:24]`

## The surge and the firing `[00:04:03]`
- As Iraq drew down, Afghanistan escalated. Obama's initial **17,000** troops became 21,000, then more. He fired top general **David McKiernan** (the first such firing since Truman/MacArthur) — his "subversion": being "the first general to admit the war… was going poorly." Bush-era language was sanitized ("global war on terror" → "overseas contingency operation"). But violence was rising, Kandahar uncaptured. `[00:04:03]`

## McChrystal and COIN `[00:07:03]`
- **May 2009** — **Gen. Stanley McChrystal** took command: ascetic (one meal a day, 4–5 hrs sleep), ex-head of the secretive **JSOC** hunter-killers in Iraq (a secret-prison/torture scandal closed because units used "battlefield pseudonyms" and "70% of computer files had been lost"). He brought "his boys" (incl. Michael Flynn). With Petraeus above him, he pushed Iraq-style **counterinsurgency (COIN)** — "less about killing the enemy, more about winning hearts and minds" by opening space for the Afghan government to deliver services. His wishlist: **60,000 more troops**, massive aid, tighter rules of engagement. `[00:07:03]`
- **Who is the enemy?** `[00:12:42]`: COIN's flaw — it presumed Afghans saw the Taliban as the enemy, when the Pashtun south/east "saw them as far less noxious than the non-Pashtun American-backed warlords." Even Karzai was "confused" ("I start hearing people in your government saying that we didn't need to kill bin Laden and Mullah Omar"). **Richard Holbrooke** (the JFK-inspired, loudmouth diplomat who'd done Dayton, aiming to be Secretary of State) and ambassador **Karl Eikenberry** both rejected COIN, begging instead to crack down on Pakistan's safe havens. `[00:12:42]`
- **Splitting the difference** `[00:15:00]`: Obama sent **30,000** troops (plus NATO) — ~100,000 US troops, 32,000 other foreign troops, and 104,000 US-paid mercenaries (more than the Soviets ever had) — with an **18-month deadline** almost no one supported (an aide "stupefied": "with that deadline, you can't use that strategy"). In public they presented a unified front (McChrystal: "convince the majority of the Afghan people that… we are going to win"). `[00:15:00]`

## Karzai's stolen election `[00:17:00]`
- **Nov 2009** — Clinton beamed at Karzai's inauguration; he had "stolen the election three months earlier" (a UN-backed probe found **1 million illegal votes**, a quarter of all cast). The Americans had soured on Karzai (Holbrooke hated him), backed his opposition — so Karzai cut deals with Fahim and Dostum, stacked the oversight commission, and "turned his back on his American patrons." He could point to denied civilian massacres (a July 2008 airstrike killed 47, mostly women and children including a bride; a Helmand strike killed up to 92, mostly children). Biden had stormed out of a dinner with Karzai; Karzai once threatened to *join the Taliban*. `[00:17:00]`
- **Kabul Bank** `[00:20:50]`: in 2010 Afghanistan's largest bank turned out to be a **$1B pyramid scheme** — shareholders included a leading warlord's brother and Karzai's brother; neither prosecuted; $300M airlifted in to cover depositors (how this happened with thousands of US advisors present "was never explained"). `[00:20:50]`

## "Money as a Weapons System" `[00:22:07]`
- COIN demanded "dump trucks of money" — the Army even published *Commander's Guide to Money as a Weapons System*; spending tripled ($6B in 2008 → $17B in 2010). The real problem was **corruption**: Afghanistan became one of the most corrupt states in the world. A "surge in wartime entrepreneurship" (Susie Hansen): Vegas-gaudy wedding halls, white SUVs, money to "overpriced contractors or corrupt officials" while clinics and roads fell apart "if they were built at all." `[00:22:07]`

## Marja and the fall of McChrystal `[00:23:44]`
- **Feb 2010** — McChrystal's showcase COIN operation at **Marja** ("we don't want Fallujah… Fallujah is not the model") — a failure; within six months the Marines were under routine Taliban assault. Petraeus collapsed at a Senate hearing (dehydration) — an omen: a week later McChrystal was out, undone by Michael Hastings's Rolling Stone profile **"The Runaway General"** (his team's on-record contempt — "Vice President Bite Me," Holbrooke "a wounded animal," Karzai "the man with a funny hat"). The gravest sin: saying Obama "didn't seem very engaged on Afghanistan." NYT reporters (Lara Logan, Geraldo) defended McChrystal; Obama accepted his resignation **June 23, 2010**. **Petraeus** took over. `[00:23:44]`

## The CIA protects the corruption `[00:30:42]`
- Former advisor **Sarah Chayes**: in 2009 an Afghan provincial police chief was gunned down by the **Kandahar Strike Force** (a CIA-run unit personally provided by **Ahmed Wali Karzai**, drug lord and presidential brother). A Karzai aide caught in a corruption scandal (Mohammad Zia Salehi) was sprung from jail — he was a **CIA bagman**. The structure: bottom-up mafia tribute (every level paying the one above; the money leaving Afghanistan to Dubai/Switzerland). Chayes: the government "was not incapable. It was performing its core function with admirable efficiency" — extraction. Afghans concluded "the Americans want the corruption"; State and the CIA pushed back on every prosecution (the CIA attending anti-corruption meetings "to learn our moves and protect its people"). **2011** — Petraeus left to head the CIA; Holbrooke (squeezed out by Petraeus's "full-spectrum COIN with a lethal edge") died of a ruptured aorta. `[00:30:42]`

## The heroin oligarchy `[00:35:40]`
- Afghanistan supplied **93% of global opium** (UN); Obama's surge couldn't (or wasn't meant to) undo it. The Counter-Narcotics minister was "one of the biggest drug traffickers in the country"; Ahmed Wali Karzai a top kingpin (killed by his own bodyguard, July 2011). The narco-state bred a hyper-rich class in "poppy palaces" (pink granite, heated indoor pools, $12,000/month) while money launderers flew suitcases of cash to Dubai. The lion's share went "not to the Taliban… but to friends of the American occupiers" — so the drug war targeted only the dealers opposed to the US (DEA officer Kirk Meyer: the national security staff "were the worst of the cockroaches… they just scuttled for the dark corners"). `[00:35:40]`

## The drone war & Raymond Davis `[00:39:00]`
- By 2010 the Taliban (regrouped in Pakistan) "were at their zenith"; more CIA **drone strikes** hit Pakistan than anywhere (≥2,000 killed; Bush had dropped prior warning to Pakistan in 2008; the CIA could strike "even when… not certain about exactly whom they were killing"). **Jan 2011** — Blackwater/CIA contractor **Raymond Davis** shot two men dead in Lahore (a consulate SUV killed a third); his car held a mask, ~100 bullets, and photos of Pakistani military sites. The incident "helped turn Pakistan against the United States" (a victim's widow took rat poison, certain a deal would free the killer — she was right: the CIA man was freed for **$2.3M** blood money). `[00:39:00]`

## Killing bin Laden `[00:43:00]`
- **May 1, 2011** — Navy SEALs (another JSOC unit) killed **bin Laden** at a compound in **Abbottabad**; celebrations erupted; *Zero Dark Thirty* (CIA-supervised) premiered 18 months later. But the odd details: the compound had no security/escape hatches and sat <1 mile from the Pakistani Military Academy. Carlotta Gall's bombshell: the **ISI ran a special desk handling bin Laden in Abbottabad** ("they hid bin Laden… to keep the war on terror on the boil and US financial assistance flowing"). Seymour **Hersh**'s 2015 LRB piece: the intel came not from torture or courier-tracking but from a **walk-in ISI officer** who took the $25M reward; the Pakistani military *was* notified and cleared the SEAL helicopters' airspace; the "treasure trove" of al-Qaeda intel was largely turned over by the Pakistanis (who razed the building). The two operations: an election-season PR campaign about Obama's "gutsy call," and a cover story over "a much more banal and ultimately ignoble truth." NBC's Andrea Mitchell defended the official line. `[00:43:00]`

## The biggest myth: the war itself `[00:54:53]`
- Clinton's address of progress stats (7.1 million students, 40% girls; infant mortality down 22%) was "based on cooked books." A SIGAR official: officials "knew that data was bad, yet bragged about the numbers anyway." No one reliably tracked Afghan casualties (the last program dropped in 2005). 2012 brought hard-to-spin scandals: Marines urinating on Taliban corpses; the Bagram Quran burnings; an Army sergeant massacring 16 Kandahar villagers; insider "green-on-blue" attacks; a suicide attack targeting Defense Secretary Panetta. With only 38% of Americans saying the war was worth it (vs. 90% early on), the White House began drawing down (~100,000 → 67,000 by mid-2012). `[00:54:53]`
- **The hollow Afghan forces** `[00:59:08]`: the police were "more of a paramilitary force than a crime-fighting agency"; the 350,000-strong army (and police) padded with tens of thousands of "ghost" personnel who didn't exist; 64,000+ Afghan uniformed personnel killed by the 2010s. `[00:59:08]`

## "End of combat" `[01:00:02]`
- **Dec 2014** — the White House announced the war was over ("Resolute Support"); Obama didn't attend the Kabul ceremony (sending a statement from Hawaii). The NATO chief's "741 million years of life" boast rested on spurious data (a 2017 audit). Troops sank to ~11,000 — but thousands of contractors/mercenaries remained, and the Pentagon "carved out numerous exceptions that made the distinctions almost meaningless" (air war and counterterror continued). Old faces endured: Dostum, Ismail Khan, Sayyaf (now an MP), the Haqqani network, Hekmatyar (head of a feared insurgent group, then pardoned in 2015 and switching sides again). `[01:00:02]`
- **Karzai out, Ghani in** `[01:04:01]`: Karzai (barred from a third term, blocking a status-of-forces deal) left with a rent-free palace and lifetime pension; **Ashraf Ghani** (ex-World Bank economist, ran with Dostum) won an even more fraudulent 2014 election. **Sept 2015** — the Taliban took Kunduz; a US gunship strafed a **Doctors Without Borders hospital** there, killing 42 ("no excuse for the attack" — the US had the coordinates). The US put the Taliban in "a nebulous new category… not necessarily the enemy," concluding the war could only end via a **power-sharing peace deal** — while **ISIS** expanded into Afghanistan (US forces could freely attack ISIS/al-Qaeda but fight the Taliban "only in self-defense"). **Jan 2017** — Obama left with the war "on autopilot"; the final drawdown never happened (~8,500 troops + mercenaries remained). `[01:04:01]`
- **Holbrooke's deathbed** `[01:09:08]`: Dec 2010 — Holbrooke raised Afghan women's rights with Biden, who erupted: "I am not sending my boy back there to risk his life on behalf of women's rights… We did it in Vietnam. Nixon and Kissinger got away with it." Holbrooke died days later; on the way into surgery (his Pakistani-led surgical team joking they'd "deal with Afghanistan"), he quipped: "See if you can take care of that, including ending the war." `[01:09:08]`
