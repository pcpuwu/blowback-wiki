## Cold open: the snake eats its tail `[00:00:05]`
- A reverse-chronological montage: the **2021 Kabul collapse** (Afghans falling from US planes) → **2001** (9/11 and Bush's strikes) → **1996** (the Taliban take three-quarters of the country; Brzezinski's "what is more important… the Taliban or the collapse of the Soviet Empire?") → **1989** (the Soviet retreat) → **1980** (Brzezinski on the Afghan border telling the mujahideen "God is on your side"). The Ouroboros: "the snake eats its tail." `[00:00:05]`
- Roadmap: a two-parter — the **1980s Soviet-Afghan War**, then the road to **9/11** and the US occupation. "Secret agents, Soviet commissars, Afghan warlords, Texas cowboys, Saudi oligarchs, and the people caught in between, in a 40-year war that may not yet be over." `[00:04:28]`

## The 2021 withdrawal `[00:07:01]`
- The withdrawal "woke Americans up" — the press screamed ("Abandoning Afghanistan is a historic mistake" — NYT; "the worst kind, avoidable" — WaPo) as if the chaos started that day, with no connection to 20 years of occupation. The questions piling up: Was this another Vietnam? Was Afghanistan "the good war" overshadowed by Iraq — or the *foundation* of the post-9/11 crusade? (Rumsfeld: "we're not running out of targets. Afghanistan is.") `[00:07:01]`

## Myth-busting: the Rambo 3 title card `[00:09:55]`
- The famous "trivia" that **Rambo 3** (1988) was dedicated "to the brave Mujahideen fighters of Afghanistan" (changed post-9/11 to "the gallant people") is **a myth** — a Photoshop hoax that fooled the internet and even academics; the original always read "gallant people." But it's "spiritually true": Rambo 3, *The Beast*, *Red Scorpion* recast America's Vietnam crimes onto the Soviets ("Russia's Vietnam"), letting Americans "finally play the anti-imperialists" only five years after their own defeat — with help from the US government and even apartheid South Africa. CIA director **Bill Casey**: "the beauty of the Afghan operation… the Russians are beating up on the little guys. We don't make it our war." `[00:09:55]`

## MacJihad — Islam as American as apple pie `[00:14:45]`
- Pakistani writer **Saadat Hasan Manto**'s 1954 *Letters to Uncle Sam* satirically advised the US to arm Islamic militants to scare off the Soviets — "if this sect of Mullahs is armed American style, then the Soviet Union will have to pick up its spittoon." Against the "McDonald's versus jihad" framing, scholar **Timothy Mitchell**'s "**MacJihad**": "the more closely a government is allied with Washington, the more Islamic its politics" (independent states — Nasser's Egypt, Ba'athist Syria — were secular; US-dependent ones claimed Islamic authority). Political Islam plays "an unacknowledged role in the making of… global capitalism" — explaining US approval of Pakistan's Taliban (for pipeline deals, "a little Saudi Arabia") and shared interests with al-Qaeda in the 90s. `[00:14:45]`

## The buried bombshells `[00:18:04]`
- Things most Americans never heard: in fall 2001, **Cheney supervised the evacuation** of hundreds of al-Qaeda/Taliban by Pakistani planes over the border. Late in the 80s jihad, the CIA ran warlords on **cross-border raids inside the USSR** itself. The "medieval" Taliban hired Washington PR (socialite **Lely Helms**, niece of ex-CIA director Richard Helms). The US cooked up **WMD allegations** against the USSR ("yellow rain") — changing the subject from America's own chemicals in Vietnam. The 9/11 fixer **Ali Mohamed** (who taught al-Qaeda kidnapping/hijacking) was a US Green Beret, FBI informant, and CIA asset. The first international warrant for **bin Laden** came not from the US but Gaddafi's Libya. And Afghanistan was transformed into the world's largest **heroin** supplier (90% at the occupation's height) — the CIA, Pakistanis, and mujahideen juiced the trade, with production peaking in *US-controlled* regions. `[00:18:04]`

## The cast `[00:23:24]`
- **Charlie Wilson** — the debauched Texas congressman ("cocaine Charlie") who turned the jihad into "a Washington cocktail circuit cause célèbre"; childhood hero Douglas MacArthur; staffed his office with "Charlie's Angels." `[00:23:24]`
- **Gus Avrakotos** — the blue-collar Greek-American CIA day-to-day manager (advised the Greek junta to "shoot the motherfucker"); trained the mujahideen in kidnapping/assassination. His bond with Wilson: "chasing pussy and killing communists." `[00:26:00]`
- **Bill Casey** — CIA director, devout Catholic, Knight of Malta, who saw political Islam and the Church as "natural allies"; cultivated Pakistan's Zia and Saudi Arabia's King Fahd. `[00:27:30]`
- **Zbigniew Brzezinski** — Carter's NSA, "the architect of the jihad," who admitted using the mujahideen (supported *before* the Soviet invasion) as an "Afghan trap" to draw the USSR into a quagmire (then testily denied "baiting" in the famous interview). `[00:28:29]`
- **The mujahideen** `[00:30:11]`: **Ahmed Shah Massoud** (the romanticized "Lion of the Panjshir," "the Parisian" — but a drug dealer); **Gulbuddin Hekmatyar** (the most notorious, >$1B in arms, threw acid in women's faces, hosted early al-Qaeda camps); **Abdul Rasul Sayyaf** (whose camps trained Ramzi Yousef and where KSM conceived 9/11). "Less traditional religious figures and more your typical mafia bosses" — warring over turf ("It's not the Soviets they're worried about. It's each other"). `[00:30:11]`
- **Osama bin Laden** `[00:34:51]`: son of Mohammed bin Laden (the Saudi construction magnate, 52 children, air-conditioned Mecca); Osama used family wealth to fund Afghan camps and became "basically a contractor for Saudi intelligence." His brother Salem invested in George W. Bush's failed **Arbusto Energy** (1977). `[00:34:51]`

## The Bush–Saudi axis `[00:38:33]`
- The US–Saudi axis "conditions everything." A 2001 Toronto Star piece: the Bush administration told the FBI to **back off investigating the bin Laden clan** — bin Laden "had powerful protectors in both Washington and Saudi Arabia." Both families invested in the **Carlyle Group** (military contractors); Kevin Phillips: no political family rivals the Bushes' "four-decade relationship with the Saudi royal family." And the reluctance to prosecute **BCCI** — the dark-money network financing crime, drugs, and the mujahideen. `[00:38:33]`
- **Sign-off** `[00:41:01]`: the war was "a propaganda war too" (Bush's "crusade"). Guests this season: Afghan activist **Malalai Joya**, Pakistani journalist **Ahmed Rashid**, and muckraker **Seymour Hersh** — "from the ancient empires to the Cold War standoff to 9/11 and beyond." `[00:41:01]`
