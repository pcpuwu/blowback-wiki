## Cold open: the Khas Uruzgan massacre `[00:00:07]`
- **Late Jan 2002** — in Khas Uruzgan, US Special Forces killed 19 men (a pro-American official and his aides) on a bad tip from a *local rival* — Afghans learned to eliminate enemies by labeling them Taliban. Another Karzai supporter lay dead with US plastic cuffs ("U.S. patent number…"). Uruzgan was punished repeatedly (AC-130s killed 54 at a wedding; six raids, 80 civilians dead, zero Taliban leaders captured). This rendered body counts and the enemy/civilian distinction "highly suspect." `[00:00:07]`

## The Axis of Evil `[00:04:48]`
- **Jan 29, 2002** — Bush's "Axis of Evil" speech labeled **Iran** (which had *helped* form the interim government) one of "the three most evil entities on the planet." The warlords exploited the US–Iran rivalry: **Ismail Khan** (warlord of the west) "fed them tidbits of misinformation" while raking in $3–5M/month in customs, running his own Taliban-style vice squads (smashing music, examining women for "virtue"). Dostum took ~40% of northern customs; Sayyaf bought a town. Many "new" warlords were ex-Taliban bought with CIA cash. `[00:04:48]`
- **"Man-love Thursday"** `[00:09:00]`: US allies practiced **bacha bazi** ("boy play") — keeping adolescent "tea boys" as sex slaves; US troops were ordered to look the other way. `[00:09:00]`

## Stingy nation-building `[00:09:59]`
- The country had "some of the worst living conditions ever recorded" while Rumsfeld's Pentagon (gearing up for Iraq) skimped. More traffic but no reliable power or safe water; Massoud "deified… in a town he destroyed." International donors underfunded reconstruction. Bush, asked to meet the general running Afghanistan: "Who is General McNeil?… I don't need to meet with him." A Green Beret: Kandahar was now "miserable rat-fuck shithole." Mullah Omar promised the US "fire, hell, and total defeat"; the OG commanders regrouped in Pakistan (Haqqani in North Waziristan, Hekmatyar back from Iran, bin Laden escaped from Tora Bora) while Rumsfeld had Franks planning the Iraq invasion. `[00:09:59]`

## The Loya Jirga & the Karzai machine `[00:13:55]`
- **June 2002** — the first post-Taliban Loya Jirga was the moment to "sweep out the warlords" — instead the cabinet was handed to Northern Alliance warlords. Karzai (after a VP's assassination) took US bodyguards. The reconstruction that happened benefited specific people: brother **Mahmoud Karzai** bought 10,000 Kandahar acres for a gated community (with a $3M US grant); brother **Ahmed Wali Karzai** was a Kandahar drug-trade wheeler-dealer *and on the CIA payroll*. Some southern Afghans wanted the Americans gone, but Halliburton, DynCorp, and Lockheed were settling in for a long war (Bagram near Herat being "ideal for monitoring the Iranian border"). The CIA built an Afghan secret police (the NDS). `[00:13:55]`

## Cash to the dictatorships `[00:17:00]`
- Central Asian handouts kept the occupation running (regional aid doubled to $440M+ in 2002; Uzbekistan got $155M, Kyrgyzstan $92M for bases). China militarized its Central Asian borders, fearing Uyghur extremists. The biggest beneficiary: **Pakistan/Musharraf** — Rumsfeld calling him "a superb partner" (echoing his old praise of Saddam: "He's a strong man… an intelligent man"). Up to 70% of $5.4B in military aid "gone missing"; Pakistan's generals were compensated for losing the Taliban so long as they fed the US al-Qaeda suspects. `[00:17:00]`

## Torture, black sites, Daniel Pearl `[00:21:00]`
- The CTC, once tasked with bin Laden, now handled "high-value targets" — pre-9/11 rendition (to Egypt, Jordan, Poland) became **black sites** (revealed 2005, from Eastern Europe to Thailand): "the most shockingly bureaucratized descent into… pseudoscience on human subjects… since MK-Ultra" (Coll). The CIA taped the torture, then destroyed the tapes in 2005. **Daniel Pearl** (WSJ) was beheaded on video near Karachi (May 2002) — some Pakistani journalists suspected ISI hardliners encouraged it to deter reporting. Musharraf kept signing US checks while banning demonstrations and courting Islamist parties — "feed the US victories against al-Qaeda, while secretly giving the Islamic militants protection." `[00:21:00]`
- **India vs. Pakistan** `[00:25:00]`: Afghanistan was a zero-sum contest with India (which poured $500M in). Afghan–Pakistan trade grew from $100M (2000) to $1.6B (2006). Musharraf's two-track policy nearly got him killed (**Dec 2003** — two assassination attempts in Rawalpindi within days; an FBI jamming device saved him). Rob Schultheis on the ground: ISI-backed villages "trying to revive al-Qaeda," money probably "from Saudi Arabia." `[00:25:00]`

## "Mission Accomplished" — and the comeback `[00:27:56]`
- **May 2003** — as Bush declared "Mission Accomplished" in Iraq, Rumsfeld declared the Afghan war "conclusively won" (Karzai's joke: "I thought you had all gone to Iraq"). An officer: "quite frankly, we were just going around killing people… and of course the Taliban would just flow right back in." US troops policed insurgents whom the *Pakistani ally* was housing, training, and funding miles away. Spring 2003 — the first major **Taliban offensive** in the south (where the Taliban remained more popular than the warlords; Afghans asked patrolling US units "what are the Russians doing back here?"). The new Afghan army "flopped from the start." `[00:27:56]`

## Malalai Joya's speech `[00:31:10]`
- **Winter 2003 Loya Jirga** — **Malalai Joya** (raised in refugee camps, an underground girls' teacher under the Taliban) was "shocked and appalled" to see warlords and war criminals in the front row (Sayyaf — "his fascist mind," who first invited bin Laden and mentored KSM; Rabbani; Dostum; Ismail Khan; Mojaddedi). In a ~90-second speech she demanded they be prosecuted — chairman Mojaddedi cut her mic; warlords shook fists ("take the pants off this whore and tie them on her head"); a widow, Aisha, shielded her with her body. The speech became international news, putting women's rights front and center (fellow feminist journalist **Zakia Zaki** of Peace Radio was later murdered by warlord-linked gunmen). Joya served as an MP under constant bodyguard, in safe houses. The Loya Jirga's concession to the religious right: the **Supreme Court** (controlled by Sayyaf) got power to review legislation — Sharia for 50% of the population. `[00:31:10]`

## The American Viceroy `[00:37:55]`
- **2004** — Doctors Without Borders withdrew after 24 years (five staff assassinated). Enter ambassador **Zalmay Khalilzad** ("Zal") — born in Afghanistan, who got his start under Brzezinski at Columbia, worked at RAND and consulted for **Unocal**, now "the most powerful man in Afghanistan" (Joya: "a wolf in the skin of a lamb… people call him a small Bush"), eclipsing Karzai. Madrid train bombings (March 2004) showed the global threat; the CIA claimed 70% of al-Qaeda leadership captured/killed (ignoring replacements). The **federally administered tribal areas** became al-Qaeda's new base (plots for London, Madrid, Bali); Haqqani organized the new **Pakistani Taliban**. `[00:37:55]`
- **The A.Q. Khan pardon** `[00:44:30]`: Pakistan made a non-NATO ally (June 2004); nuclear scientist **A.Q. Khan** (caught selling nuclear tech) took the fall as "one bad apple" — Musharraf pardoned him, Bush let Musharraf off the hook. **Oct 2004** — Karzai elected president (55%, 15 candidates withdrawing over fraud); bin Laden released his boldest tape (mocking Bush's "goat story" response on 9/11), well-timed to help Bush win re-election. `[00:44:30]`

## The contractor bonanza `[00:47:01]`
- The era of the **contractor** — Northrop, Blackwater, Raytheon, Boeing, Lockheed, General Dynamics, all paid millions; Congress used "emergency funding that circumvented the normal budget process" (Gates's "culture of endless money"; ≥10% kept in classified accounts). Louis Berger won contracts for 96 clinics/schools — built 9 clinics and 2 schools (execs later charged with fraud). The logistics contract (LOGCAP) went to **KBR**, a **Halliburton** subsidiary — Cheney had *invented* LOGCAP as Defense Secretary in 1992. A December 2001 Pentagon contract saved Halliburton from possible asbestos-driven bankruptcy: "a sweetheart contract. There's no other word for it." `[00:47:01]`

## The heroin explosion `[00:56:00]` *(narrated ~00:56)*
- By 2004 opium production was **20× the Taliban-era level** (Hersh), concentrated in areas with a major US military presence; by 2006, Afghanistan was **92% of global opium**. The Taliban's share was "only about a tenth" — the bulk went to "the Karzai machine and the warlords associated with it" (Britain's ex-ambassador Craig Murray: "the four largest players… are all senior members of the Afghan government"). 9 tons of opium found in Helmand governor Akhundzada's office (a Karzai friend — removed, then given a parliament seat); Ahmed Wali Karzai a notorious player. US anti-drug strategy targeted *only* traffickers supporting the insurgents — to keep the trade "under control of those traffickers who are allies of the state security apparatus and/or the CIA." `[00:56:00]`

## The torture regime `[00:56:00]` *(narrated ~00:56–01:01)*
- The Red Cross's plea for humane treatment went unanswered; all prisoners became "illegal enemy combatants" (Gonzales calling Geneva "quaint") — part of Rumsfeld/Cheney's COG framework. The border became "an immense human bazaar"; boys 13–16 and men over 80 ended up in Guantanamo. Taxi driver **Dilawar**, detained over a broken walkie-talkie, was hooded and chained at Bagram and **found dead Dec 10, 2002** (homicide by beating; no charges). Bush's 2003 State of the Union on suspects who "met a different fate… no longer a problem." CIA "ghost detainees" disappeared entirely; contractor **Jonathan Idema** ran a private prison (three dead Afghans hanging in his home), disavowed and jailed. `[00:56:00]`

## The deepening war (2006–08) `[01:01:21]`
- Suicide bombing ("Mullah Omar's missiles") and IEDs surged (530 in 2005 → ~1,300 in 2006); the ISI tipped off Hekmatyar's bombing cell and killed the NATO informant. Washington belatedly raised aid ($3.2B, then doubled; $10.1B for the army/police in 2007) — too little, too late. By the Bush years' end Afghanistan was 5th-from-last on the UN Human Development Index (12% female literacy, life expectancy 43). Melvin Goodman: "the Soviet Union actually built more hydroelectric dams, tunnels, and bridges in Afghanistan than the United States." `[01:01:21]`
- **Women's rights as cover** `[01:02:30]`: Laura Bush's radio address notwithstanding, women's liberation was "mostly theoretical" (the 2004 constitution's equality clause undercut by a corrupt Sharia/tribal judiciary). Joya: "a shameless lie… a catastrophic situation of women was a very good excuse for US and NATO to occupy Afghanistan." `[01:02:30]`
- **Benazir Bhutto & the Cheney attack** `[01:06:50]`: **Dec 2007** — Benazir Bhutto assassinated (her son blamed Musharraf). Earlier in 2007 a suicide bomber hit Bagram during a **Cheney** visit (killing ~23, incl. 2 Americans) — the Taliban's claim that Cheney was the target was publicly dismissed as "absurd" but privately known to be true ("if he had waited 30 minutes more…"). "But even [Bush] would have to take second place to the next American administration." `[01:06:50]`
