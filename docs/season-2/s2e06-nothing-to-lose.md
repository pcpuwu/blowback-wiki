## Cold open `[00:00:00]`
- JFK's report on the Vienna summit — the new threat of "wars of liberation," and Khrushchev's point (which JFK concedes) that **not every uprising is communist-inspired**; communists "did not create the conditions." `[00:00:00]`

## The fallout & "nothing to lose" `[00:02:37]`
- A furious, humiliated JFK declared the "final chapter on Cuba had not been written"; Cuba became "the top priority… all else is secondary." RFK's note: "we have nothing to lose." JFK blamed the CIA ("CIA bastards") and Joint Chiefs while keeping ~80% approval — though a majority of Americans *opposed* invading. `[00:02:37]`
- At the **ASNE speech** he called for press restraint and warned "our restraint is not inexhaustible." Per Cuban historian **Jesús Arboleya**, the new strategy needed: manufactured instability, a Cold-War (not anti-colonial) framing, and regional isolation — cloaked in US "revolutionary" language. Kornbluh: JFK said there were "sobering lessons" and then learned none, walking right back into covert war for revenge. `[00:06:30]`

## The mafia scandal & RFK's compromise `[00:12:16]`
- The CIA-mafia plot nearly went public: **Giancana**, jealous over Phyllis McGuire, had the CIA's cutout **Maheu** wiretap comedian Dan Rowan in Las Vegas — the bug was caught, risking a trial that would expose everything. This compromised **RFK** (the anti-mob crusader pursuing Carlos Marcello), whose Justice Dept also met mob gun-runner **Norman Rothman** (who offered Cuba sabotage assets for dropped charges). `[00:12:16]`
- **The Taylor Review** (June 1961) `[00:17:48]`: a sham post-mortem (RFK lead questioner; Dulles reviewing his own operation; collective "amnesia" about the assassination plots) whose real purpose was to argue the US and the revolution could never coexist — and to set up a new executive-branch-run program with RFK as point man. `[00:17:48]`

## Vienna & Berlin `[00:20:45]`
- **June 3, 1961** — at Vienna, Khrushchev went on the offensive: the **Jupiter missiles** in Turkey/Italy (linking them to Cuba — "what if the USSR invaded Turkey?"), **Berlin** (a peace treaty to remove the West Berlin "splinter"), and "**Castro is not a communist, but US policy can make him one**." JFK called it "the worst thing in my life" and turned to **Vietnam** to prove US credibility. `[00:20:45]`

## Reshuffling the CIA `[00:24:33]`
- **Bissell out → Richard Helms in** (a secrecy hawk: "secret from inception to eternity"). The **Kirkpatrick IG report** (kept secret ~40 years) found Bissell grossly incompetent and Dulles culpable. Bissell, chewed out by the Kennedys, later told the Church Committee there was "no assassination activity" — false (the **Amblood** plot, a Gagarin-visit plot, a bazooka plot, all failed in 1961). `[00:24:33]`
- The abandoned exiles: pilot Eddie Ferrer got $300 and an unsigned letter; CRC's Miró Cardona was promised the effort would continue. `[00:33:48]`

## Operation Mongoose `[00:35:02]`
- Run by **Edward Lansdale** (the counterinsurgency "James Bond," hated inside the CIA) under the **Special Group Augmented** (with RFK). New cast: CIA director **McCone**, **Helms**, and field man **William Harvey** (of the ZR/RIFLE assassination program, now also Task Force W). The budget jumped **$10M → $100M**; the Miami station **JMWAVE** became the largest CIA station in the world (~500 staff, ~5,000 personnel, the CIA's own navy). `[00:35:02]`
- Dummy corporations, commando raids (Rip Robertson paid $100 for "the ear of a Cuban" — a man brought two), and a six-phase plan ending in an **overt US invasion scheduled for Oct 1962**. The Joint Chiefs even drafted **Operation Northwoods** — false-flag terror (blow up a US ship at Guantánamo, fake a downed airliner, a "Cuban" terror campaign in US cities) to justify invasion — officially rejected. Plus Operations Smasher/Breakup/**Dirty Trick** (blame Cuba if John Glenn's flight failed)/**Bingo**. `[00:41:00]`
- **The diplomatic/economic war** `[00:48:00]`: Jan 1962, Rusk got the **OAS to expel Cuba** (on forged CIA documents; Haiti's deciding vote bought with an airport). JFK added sanctions (~$35M) — first dispatching Pierre Salinger to buy him **thousands of Cuban cigars**. Kornbluh: the largest, most sustained effort to wreck Cuba's economy. `[00:48:00]`

## Toward the brink `[00:51:44]`
- **Nov 1961** — JFK to NYT's Tad Szulc: "what would you think if I ordered Castro to be assassinated?" (Goodwin suspected him of distancing himself / using Szulc). RFK then quashed the Giancana wiretap case; State Dept drew up a post-assassination plan. CIA estimates: most Cubans still backed the revolution, so the "contingency" to kill Castro was seen as the best shot. Harvey studied MI5's (unused) plan to nerve-gas Nasser and met Roselli. `[00:51:44]`
- **The spiral to the Missile Crisis** `[00:56:05]`: Cuba turned again to the USSR (Sept 1961 arms); Castro declared himself Marxist-Leninist, which *worried* Moscow. **Feb 1962** — JFK warned Khrushchev (via his son-in-law) that "Cuba will be the main problem… we'll have to do something." Lansdale's memo aimed for a revolt by **Oct 1962**; US invasion exercises (Quick Kick, Whiplash — JFK watched one with the **Shah of Iran**); Alpha 66's deadly raid. Cuba and the USSR concluded the US was about to invade — and "**Khrushchev believed he had an answer.**" `[00:56:05]`
