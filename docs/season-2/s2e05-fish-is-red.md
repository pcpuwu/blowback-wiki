## Cold open `[00:00:08]`
- **2019** — Trump and Pence honor Bay of Pigs veterans at the White House ("it will always be ¡Cuba Libre!") — the invasion still a live political totem. `[00:00:08]`

## The stakes & the hubris `[00:02:28]`
- The Bay of Pigs meant different things to each player: for the CIA, proof it could remake the world; for the Batista-heavy Brigade 2506, taking their country back; for JFK, a "contained" problem ("how could I have been so stupid?"); for Cuba, the long-awaited American attack. `[00:02:28]`
- **Feb 1961** — at the exclusive **Alibi Club** dinner, Bissell introduced himself as "your man-eating shark" — a hit with JFK's set. His earlier boast ("there will be no communist government in Latin America while I am DDP") captured the operation's fatal flaw: **imperial hubris**. Per Peter Kornbluh, the CIA never learned how *lucky* it had gotten in Guatemala 1954, and assumed Castro would fold like Árbenz. `[00:04:39]`
- **Fidel's home turf** `[00:08:01]`: Castro had "discovered" the Bay of Pigs (the Zapata swamp) as a personal project *two years before the Americans* — fishing there, building a road for the charcoal-makers, spending Christmas 1959 with villagers. The CIA picked his backyard. `[00:08:01]`

## The poison that didn't reach him `[00:10:25]`
- The botulinum pills reached **Juan Orta** (inside Castro's office) via Giancana/Roselli — but Orta lost his position and fled, returning the pills. The mob then turned to **Tony Varona** (with Trafficante cash) and a restaurant contact; CIA handler O'Connell passed new pills. All that was needed was the green light. `[00:10:25]`

## JFK signs off `[00:12:36]`
- **Early April 1961** — JFK fretted about "noise"; Bissell falsely assured him the Cuban air force could be knocked out, that ~25% would rise up, and that Castro didn't know. To reduce noise, the landing was shifted from Trinidad to the isolated **Bay of Pigs** — which doomed it. After an Easter weekend with his anti-communist father and ex-ambassador Earl Smith, and the **April 3 white paper** (Schlesinger), JFK was sold — also by Dulles's "**disposal problem**" (what to do with 1,400 trained exiles if you cancel). A **Tad Szulc NYT** story (April 6) was personally watered down by JFK. `[00:12:36]`
- **Fidel already knew** `[00:18:15]`: US bombings intensified; tipped by Soviet intel and his own agents, Fidel warned of the coming attack on **April 8**; JFK publicly denied any invasion on **April 12** — which Fidel took as a confession. `[00:18:15]`

## The invasion `[00:19:44]`
- Ships left **April 11–13**; exiles were promised air cover. Somoza saw off the Nicaragua group ("bring me a couple hairs from Castro's beard"). `[00:19:44]`
- **April 15** — B-26 raids failed to destroy the Cuban air force; the fake "defector" cover story (freshly-painted planes landing in Florida) collapsed within minutes — humiliating **Adlai Stevenson**, who unknowingly defended it at the UN with fake photos while Cuba's **Raúl Roa** denounced the plot. A machine-gun accident killed a man on deck; the diversionary landings failed. `[00:24:51]`
- **April 16** — Radio Swan broadcast the rise-up signal ("the fish is red"); Fidel mobilized the **CDRs** (rounding up potential collaborators) and the **National Militia** (interviews: Raúl Roa Jr.; 14-year-old Marta washing militiamen's feet). JFK gave the formal go-ahead. At the Colón cemetery, after a funeral for bombing victims, **Fidel declared the revolution socialist**: "we have carried out a socialist revolution right under the nose of the United States." `[00:24:51]`
- **The landing (April 17)** `[00:30:08]`: nothing went right — bright "Coney Island" beach lights, coral reefs the CIA had mistaken for seaweed, the still-operational Cuban air force (Batista-era **T-33 jets**) sinking the supply ships in shark-infested water. By noon Fidel was shouting "we've already won the war." Toll: **161 Cuban, 107 brigade dead; ~1,180 brigade captured** (the whole leadership). `[00:30:08]`

## The "canceled airstrike" myth `[00:33:28]`
- Bundy: "I guess Che learned more from Guatemala than we did." After the cover story blew, Rusk convinced JFK to **cancel the second airstrike** — enraging the CIA/exiles (Gen. Doster: "there goes the whole fucking war"; a pilot: "dogs obey"). `[00:33:28]`
- **But it's a myth** `[00:38:00]`: per Kornbluh — and Fidel himself (at the 40th-anniversary meeting) — Castro had **moved the planes** and put pilots on alert; a second strike would have hit empty airfields. The real flaw was the contradiction between **plausible deniability and success**: Dulles and Bissell privately bet JFK would commit US military "when the chips were down." `[00:38:00]`
- Khrushchev pledged solidarity; JFK kept denying intervention; a final April 19 bombing run (Alabama Air Guard) missed its rendezvous (and its napalm). **April 20** JFK tacitly admitted involvement; **April 21** — "victory has a hundred fathers and defeat is an orphan." `[00:42:09]`

## The assassination that didn't happen `[00:46:03]`
- The Castro hit never fired: Varona was **sequestered by other CIA agents** (to keep him from leaking) and couldn't signal his contact. Per Jake Esterline (to Kornbluh), invasion funds were funneled to the mafia for a "**magic bullet**" decapitation meant to precede the landing; the Cubans recovered the pills stuck to an icebox's coils. Maheu: "taking out Castro was part of the invasion plan." Dulles on Meet the Press denied expecting an uprising — he'd hoped for "other developments." `[00:46:03]`

## Aftermath `[00:50:53]`
- **Nixon's memoir**: JFK called Nixon for support; asked "what would you do?", Nixon said "find a legal cover and go in" — but JFK feared Khrushchev would "move on Berlin." JFK: "foreign affairs is the only important issue for a president… who gives a shit if the minimum wage is $1.25?" `[00:50:53]`
- **Che's olive branch** `[00:55:30]`: **Aug 1961**, at Punta del Este, Che sent JFK aide Richard Goodwin a box of cigars and an offer — compensate the expropriated owners (in trade), make no political alliance with the East, never attack Guantanamo, even curb revolutionary activity abroad — *and thanked the US for the invasion* (which let Cuba "consolidate… from an aggrieved little country to an equal"). Washington read the offer as **weakness** and resolved to "get it right" next time. `[00:55:30]`
- Brigade 2506 veterans would become notorious US-backed terrorists (and some, business/political leaders). `[00:55:30]`
