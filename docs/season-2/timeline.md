# Blowback Season 2 — Master Timeline (The US War on Cuba)

> A single **real-world chronological spine** across all 10 episodes. Each per-episode note (`s2eNN-*.md`) walks its episode in *narration* order; this file reorders by date. `(E#)` = the episode(s) that cover it. Sources: [`s2-sources.md`](s2-sources.md).

## Colonial roots → republic (1500s–1950s) — *E2*
- **16th c.** — Spain colonizes Cuba; near-genocide of the Taíno (Hatuey).
- **1800s** — French refugees from the Haitian Revolution build a slave-sugar economy ("the greatest slave-importing colony" of the Spanish Empire); US politicians covet Cuba (Jefferson, Douglas; a $100M offer in 1848).
- **1895** — final independence war; **José Martí** dies; Spanish concentration camps.
- **Feb 1898** — the **USS Maine** explodes → the **Spanish-American War**; US takes Cuba, PR, Guam, Philippines.
- **1902** — the neocolony: the **Platt Amendment**, Guantánamo, US-owned sugar; Marines return 1906/1912/1917.
- **1920s–30s** — Machado dictatorship → fall (1933) → **Batista** rises; the "Dance of the Millions" sugar boom and bust.
- **1940s–50s** — Batista + the **mafia** (Lansky, Luciano, Trafficante); "gangsterismo"; mass poverty.

## The Revolution (1953–1959) — *E2*
- **July 26, 1953** — Fidel's failed **Moncada** attack → "History Will Absolve Me" → prison → 1955 release → Mexico (meets Che).
- **Dec 2, 1956** — the **Granma** landing disaster (82 → ~12) → the Sierra Maestra guerrilla war.
- **1957–58** — guerrilla victories + urban underground (Frank País killed July 1957); the **Battle of Santa Clara** (Dec 1958).
- **Jan 1, 1959** — **Batista flees**; the revolution triumphs.

## Radicalization & rupture (1959–1960) — *E1, E3*
- **1959** — war-crimes trials; **Fidel's US trip** (Nixon: "communist"); land-reform debates.
- **May 17, 1959** — the **Agrarian Reform Law** → US hostility hardens; the Miami counter-revolution begins.
- **Oct 1959** — Soviet Alexiev arrives; **Oct 31** US/CIA program to undermine Castro.
- **March 4, 1960** — the **La Coubre** explosion (the iconic Che photo). Soviet trade/oil deal; US refiners refuse → Cuba nationalizes; **July** US cancels the sugar quota → USSR buys it.
- **Oct 19, 1960** — the **US embargo**. Sept 1960 — Fidel's **Harlem** trip (Malcolm X, Khrushchev). *(E4)*
- **Operation Peter Pan** (~14,000 children); CIA-mafia poison plots begin. *(E3)*

## The Bay of Pigs (1960–1961) — *E4, E5*
- **Nov 1960** — JFK elected (having out-Cuba'd Nixon); Brigade 2506 trains in Guatemala.
- **Jan 3, 1961** — Eisenhower severs relations.
- **April 17, 1961** — the **Bay of Pigs** invasion fails (the "canceled airstrike" myth; the assassination that misfired); Fidel declares the revolution **socialist**. **Aug 1961** — Che's rejected olive branch to Goodwin.

## Operation Mongoose (1961–1962) — *E6*
- The Taylor Review; **Vienna summit** (June 1961, the Jupiters & Berlin); Bissell out → Helms in.
- **Late 1961** — **Operation Mongoose** launches (Lansdale, RFK, William Harvey; JMWAVE; Operation Northwoods drafted/rejected).
- **Jan 1962** — OAS expels Cuba; sanctions tighten. The DRE's Aug 1962 Miramar raid.

## The Missile Crisis (1962) — *E7, E8*
- **Spring/May 1962** — Khrushchev conceives **Operation Anadyr** (secret missiles to Cuba); Castro accepts. **Oct 4** — first warheads arrive.
- **Oct 14** — U-2 photos discover the missiles; **Oct 16** — ExComm forms.
- **Oct 22** — JFK's **quarantine** speech. **Oct 27 ("Black Saturday")** — U-2 shot down; the B-59 submarine near-launch (**Arkhipov**).
- **Oct 28** — the deal: missiles out for a non-invasion pledge + the **secret Jupiter trade**. Tactical nukes leave Christmas Day 1962.

## The lost peace & the assassination (1963) — *E9*
- **1963** — JFK's two tracks: covert war (DRE, Alpha 66) vs. a secret **back channel** to normalize relations (Lisa Howard, Donovan, Atwood, Jean Daniel); the Limited Test Ban Treaty.
- **Nov 18, 1963** — "contact has been established" with Havana. **Nov 22, 1963** — **JFK assassinated**; Oswald (with deep DRE/CIA/mob threads) killed by Ruby; the Warren Commission's lone-gunman verdict.

## The long aftermath (1964–2021) — *E10*
- **1964–68** — the back channel dies under LBJ; Khrushchev ousted (1964); Che executed in Bolivia (1967); RFK assassinated (1968).
- **1970s** — Church/HSCA investigations (HSCA: "probably a conspiracy"); the mob witnesses die.
- **1975** — Cuba's **Angola** intervention (vs. apartheid South Africa). Decades of exile terror (Flight 455, 1976; Havana bombings, 1997).
- **1991** — Soviet collapse → the "**special period**"; the US keeps the embargo. Brothers to the Rescue shootdown (1996) → Helms-Burton.
- **2015–16** — Obama's thaw (embassy reopens; the embargo stays). **Nov 25, 2016** — **Fidel dies** (outlasting 9 presidents, ~638 assassination attempts). Trump re-tightens; Biden keeps it; 2021 protests; Díaz-Canel leads a post-revolution generation.
