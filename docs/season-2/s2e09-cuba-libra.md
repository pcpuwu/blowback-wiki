# Blowback S2E9 — "Cuba Libra"

> 📄 **[Read the full transcript](transcripts/s2e09-cuba-libra.md)**


> **Hosts:** Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · **Runtime:** ~1:23:00
> **Thesis:** 1963 — JFK runs two contradictory "tracks" on Cuba (covert war vs. a secret push to normalize relations), and just as the back channel to Castro is bearing fruit, JFK is assassinated — with the CIA-funded exile group **DRE** and a web of intelligence/mob connections threaded all through Lee Harvey Oswald's story.
>
> _Narration order; `[hh:mm:ss]` = audio jump point; real dates in **bold**. Obvious speech-to-text slips corrected against context._

## Cold open & the two tracks `[00:00:00]`
- **Spring 1963** — Lisa Howard's ABC interview with an optimistic Fidel ("we are merely… exercising our right to self-determination"). JFK ran two tracks at once: the **American University "peace" speech** (June 10, 1963 — "not a Pax Americana… genuine peace") vs. the Cold-War "**Ich bin ein Berliner**" (June 26). Both coexisted in 1963 — but couldn't forever. `[00:00:00]`

## Spinning the crisis `[00:04:43]`
- JFK's poll numbers jumped post-crisis; his team planted an official history (via Charles Bartlett) that **blamed Adlai Stevenson** for the Jupiter trade (to bury the trade itself) and burned the Soviet back-channel spy **Bolshakov**. `[00:04:43]`

## The DRE won't quit; Mongoose winds down `[00:06:15]`
- The CIA-funded **DRE** ran a "missiles hidden in caves" press offensive (Nov 1962) to keep the crisis alive and force a US invasion; JFK ordered exile refugees interrogated and the stories muzzled. **Operation Mongoose was shuttered (Jan 1963)**; Harvey's memo concluded commando ops were now pointless (implying an assassination-triggered coup was the path). Helms took an unusual meeting with the DRE's Rocha — not to rein them in, but to give them a new handler (**Walter Newby**); the DRE only got louder. `[00:09:00]`

## The prisoner deal & first diplomacy `[00:12:22]`
- **Dec 1962** — diplomat **James Donovan** negotiated the release of the **1,113 Bay of Pigs prisoners** for **$53M** in food/medicine; they were home by Christmas Eve. At the Dec 29 **Orange Bowl**, JFK took a hard line while the Miami-Cuban crowd chanted "*guerra*" (war). Donovan kept returning (Fidel led students chanting "Viva Donovan"); his porcupine joke ("very carefully") opened the first serious talk of restoring relations; he freed 27 more prisoners (incl. 3 CIA agents). `[00:12:22]`

## Cracking down on the exiles `[00:18:53]`
- **March 1963** — Alpha 66 (Antonio Veciana, handled by CIA's "**Maurice Bishop**") staged a raid to embarrass Kennedy. JFK's payback: a **March 31 crackdown** restricting 18 exiles (Veciana, DRE's Rocha and Borja). Miró Cardona resigned the CRC (April); the exile community boiled with resentment. Yet the DRE's $50k/month budget stayed intact and Newby was promoted to chief of psyops. `[00:18:53]`

## The peace track matures `[00:22:49]`
- Lisa Howard's May 1963 special ("Castro would like to talk with Kennedy"). Khrushchev patched things up with Castro (May Day in Moscow; the **Sino-Soviet split** pushing them together). The **American University speech** (recognizing the USSR's 20M WWII dead) led to the **Limited Test Ban Treaty** (signed July, ratified Oct 1963). `[00:22:49]`
- **The back channel** `[00:31:00]`: Lisa Howard → diplomat **William Atwood** → JFK greenlights contact with Cuba's UN ambassador **Lechuga** (Sept 1963). JFK also used French journalist **Jean Daniel** (Oct 24 — JFK admitted no country was more economically humiliated than Cuba "owing to my country's policies… we shall have to pay for those sins"). **Nov 19** Daniel relayed it to an overjoyed Fidel ("Kennedy still has the possibility of becoming… the greatest president") — even as, Fidel later noted, a CIA agent was handing over a **poison pen** to kill him that same day. **Nov 18, midnight** — Atwood phoned Castro's aide Vallejo; "**contact has been established.**" `[00:31:00]`

## The assassination `[00:39:08]`
- (Earlier in Nov, threats in **Chicago** — agent Abraham Bolden's report; the arrest of Thomas Vallee.) **Nov 22, 1963** — JFK is killed in Dallas; Officer Tippit shot; **Oswald** arrested; **Jack Ruby shoots Oswald** live on TV (Nov 24). Hoover briefs LBJ; the **Katzenbach memo** sets the line ("the public must be satisfied that Oswald was the assassin… cut off speculation… rebut any conspiracy"); the **Warren Commission** (with **Allen Dulles** on it) delivers exactly that: lone gunman. (Tink Thompson's forensic work later challenged the single-shooter finding.) `[00:39:08]`

## The webs `[00:53:00]`
- **Jack Ruby** — a mob-connected Dallas club owner (ties to Trafficante; deep IRS debt; calling mobsters before the shooting). `[00:53:00]`
- **Oswald's story** `[00:56:31]`: Civil Air Patrol (photographed with Marcello's pilot David Ferrie); Marines → radar tech; a 1959 "defection" to the USSR (oddly easy return, even a US loan); the anti-Soviet émigré scene in Dallas (patron **George de Mohrenschildt**); **Ruth Paine** (CIA-adjacent) who housed Marina and got him the **Texas Book Depository** job. In New Orleans (1963): ties to mob bookie uncle and spook-for-hire **Guy Banister**; a staged confrontation with the DRE's **Carlos Bringuier** while leafleting for the **Fair Play for Cuba Committee** (whose stamped address shared a building with a CIA front) — building Oswald's public **"legend"** as a pro-Castro communist (Jefferson Morley). **Mexico City** (Sept 1963): the Cuban consulate/Soviet embassy visits where the **tapes and photos don't match Oswald** (David Atlee Phillips claimed the cameras "malfunctioned"). `[00:56:31]`
- **The cover-up** `[01:16:00]`: the DRE's handler **Walter Newby** was really CIA officer **George Joannides** — who was *also* made the CIA's liaison to the 1970s House Select Committee on Assassinations (a material witness controlling the evidence flow). After JFK was shot, Joannides told the DRE to "go public" — producing the **first conspiracy theory in print** (pro-Castro Oswald) before JFK was even buried. And per Veciana, **"Maurice Bishop" was David Atlee Phillips**, whom Veciana saw with **Oswald in Dallas, 1963**. `[01:16:00]`
- **Fidel's response**: he rebuked anyone celebrating the murder, calling it "a plot against peace… a sinister conspiracy." Marta: her class first thought "he deserved it," until a professor warned "**something even worse is coming to the United States.**" `[01:20:00]`
