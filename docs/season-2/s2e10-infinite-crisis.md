# Blowback S2E10 — "Infinite Crisis"

> **Hosts:** Brendan James & Noah Kulwin · **Runtime:** 1:18:48 · **(Season 2 finale)**
> **Thesis:** The aftermath of the missile crisis and JFK's death — the lost chance at normalization — then a decades-long speed-run of the US war on Cuba (Che's death, the mob's deaths, Angola, the "special period," the Bush family's Cuba ties) through to Obama's thaw, Fidel's death, and the present.

## Cold open `[00:00:00]`
- Reagan praising Central American "democracy" (Guatemala's death squads killed ~200,000); a 1991 Bush official calling Cuba "the last dictatorship in the hemisphere." `[00:00:00]`

## The lost peace (1963–65) `[00:06:33]`
- JFK's back channel (Lisa Howard / William Atwood) died with him. Under LBJ, the **Katzenbach memo** locked in the lone-gunman line; aides warned LBJ couldn't risk looking "soft" (and Oswald's "pro-Castro" image made rapprochement harder). LBJ got West Germany to join the embargo. **Lisa Howard**'s Feb 1964 Havana interview (Fidel: "we can live in peace… on the base of respect for sovereignty"); the two were having an affair. Howard, cut out after introducing Che to Sen. McCarthy and after starting a "Democrats for Keating" group (with Gore Vidal) out of hatred for RFK, was fired by ABC and died by suicide in 1965. `[00:06:33]`
- **Khrushchev ousted** (Oct 1964; memoirs: the crisis was "a brilliant success… without firing a shot"; died 1971). Soviet-Cuba ties stayed close (~$1.5B military aid) but cooler at the top; Castro criticized the USSR over Vietnam — "nobody's pawns." `[00:19:46]`

## The 1960s wind-down `[00:25:04]`
- **1965** — RFK and Rusk were spat on in Latin America; RFK's mea culpa: "what brought about Castro… was our support of Batista." The **Cuban Adjustment Act** / boatlift brought 260,000+ to the US. **Jack Ruby** was convicted (1964), declared insane by MK-Ultra psychiatrist **Jolly West**, and died in 1967. **Che** left Cuba (1965) for the Congo, then Bolivia, where CIA man **Félix Rodríguez** oversaw his capture and execution (Oct 1967). **RFK assassinated** June 1968 ("we don't want another Oswald"). The Fair Play for Cuba Committee was infiltrated to death (founder Robert Taber's *War of the Flea* was later reprinted as a US counterinsurgency manual after 9/11). `[00:25:04]`

## The reckonings that weren't `[00:32:00]`
- The 1970s committees (Church, Pike, **HSCA**) threatened the CIA; Helms got a slap-on-the-wrist plea deal (for lying about Allende); the HSCA concluded JFK was "**probably… assassinated as the result of a conspiracy**." The mob figures died conveniently: **Roselli** (1976, body in an oil drum, before his testimony), **Giancana** (1975, before the Church Committee), **Trafficante** (deathbed: "we should have killed Bobby"); **Marcello** died free in 1993. `[00:32:00]`

## Decades of war (1970s–2000s) `[00:36:00]`
- Tentative diplomacy under Ford (LaGuardia cafeteria talks) and Carter (derailed by **Brzezinski** over Cuba's **Angola** intervention — 1975, against apartheid South Africa and the US). Continued terror: African swine fever (1971); **CORU**'s bombing of **Cubana Flight 455** (1976, 48 killed; Posada Carriles/Orlando Bosch). Reagan's Cuba obsession (Sandinistas; Haig's "I'll turn that island into a parking lot"); Grenada (1983); the Mariel boatlift (1980); the Cuba lobby (CANF, Radio/TV Martí). `[00:36:00]`
- **The Soviet collapse (1991)** `[00:46:00]` — catastrophe for Cuba: exports/imports fell ~80%, calorie intake plummeted (the "**special period**"), forcing partial market-opening and rising inequality. The US kept the embargo hoping for collapse (a 1990s-Iraq parallel; some US press cynically framed the hunger as "less obesity"). **Brothers to the Rescue** (Basulto) → Cuba shot down two planes (1996) → **Helms-Burton** sanctions (Clinton). 1997 Havana hotel bombings (Posada Carriles: "I sleep like a baby"). The **Cuban Five** arrested (1998). The **Elián González** saga (1999–2000). `[00:46:00]`
- **GWB** folded Cuba into the war on terror (Bolton's 2002 bioweapons claim — pushed by **Judy Miller** again; Powell blocked Bolton's testimony) and created the **Commission for Assistance to a Free Cuba** (2003). Posada Carriles was pardoned (2004). `[00:51:30]`

## The Bush family & the George Bush memo `[00:55:00]`
- **Jeb Bush** built his Miami career on Cuban-American ties and intervened (1989) to halt the deportation of terrorist **Orlando Bosch**. And the **George Bush/CIA memo**: historian Joseph McBride found a **Nov 29, 1963** Hoover memo briefing "**Mr. George Bush of the CIA**" on Cuban exile reactions to the assassination — which the CIA, 10 days after McBride's story, attributed to a *different* "George William Bush" (a lowly map analyst who denied ever getting such a briefing). `[00:55:00]`

## Thaw, death, and the present `[01:04:00]`
- **Obama** reopened the Havana embassy (2015) and visited Cuba (2016 — "I have come to bury the last remnant of the Cold War in the Americas"); the embargo stayed. Fidel had retired (2008) and **died Nov 25, 2016** — having outlasted nine US presidents and as many as **638 assassination attempts** (Samuel Farber, a critic: he "seized Cuba's sovereignty back from the Americans"). **Trump** re-tightened sanctions and re-added Cuba to the terror list; **Biden** kept them. The **2021 protests** (amid the supercharged sanctions + COVID) were covered as a freedom uprising — omitting the counter-protests and the inorganic social-media campaigns, and a media push for "failed state" framing / military intervention that didn't pan out. **Raúl Castro** stepped down; **Díaz-Canel** leads a post-revolution generation mandated (2019 referendum) to preserve socialism while reforming. `[01:04:00]`
- Closes with Marta: Americans don't know what the embargo does — but "seeing is believing"; visitors are convinced in a week, "and then afterwards they forgot." `[01:08:00]`
