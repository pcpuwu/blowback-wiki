## Cold open: the war never stopped `[00:00:01]`
- A montage of recent US hostility: Bush's **Oct 10, 2003** "Commission for Assistance to a Free Cuba" (co-chaired by Colin Powell); **John Bolton**'s 2002 Heritage Foundation claim that Cuba had a bioweapons program (which Powell wouldn't let him testify on under oath); the bipartisan demonization of Cuba — establishing that the war on Cuba is ongoing, not history. `[00:00:01]`

## Why Cuba `[00:06:46]`
- Like Iraq in Season 1, Cuba has been **demonized for decades**. The "canned" American story: a popular revolution betrayed by its leaders, turning the island into a backward Soviet-satellite nightmare that makes everyone poor and drives them to flee to America. `[00:11:54]`
- This view is bipartisan — pushed by Republicans *and* Democrats (Bob Menendez, the 2020-primary attacks on **Bernie Sanders** for praising Cuba's literacy program, the panic over Karen Bass and Raphael Warnock having any Cuba ties). The hosts' frame: the war on Cuba is "the story of how we couldn't tolerate anybody who wanted to dare a different dream than the American one," 90 miles offshore. `[00:11:54]`
- **The reality the US channels omit** `[00:17:09]`: per the UN World Food Programme, the revolution "largely eradicated poverty and hunger"; it eliminated illiteracy in record time and (per scholar Alejandro de la Fuente) dismantled key pillars of racial inequality; Cuba's **internationalism** (36,000 troops to Angola in 1975, more for the decisive 1988 battle of Cuito Cuanavale) helped end **apartheid** — which is why **Mandela** was Fidel's close friend; and Cuba's medical achievements (first nation to eliminate mother-to-child HIV/syphilis transmission, free doctors sent worldwide, homegrown COVID vaccines). All under 60 years of embargo. `[00:17:09]`
- **The catalog of US aggressions** `[00:20:48]`: introducing African swine fever into Cuba (1971, per the SF Chronicle); the Miami-run terror campaign; **Operation Peter Pan** (a CIA psyop that removed ~14,000 Cuban children); 600+ assassination attempts on Castro; the murder of literacy volunteers; and the **embargo** (since JFK in 1962) — whose human cost even "good liberals" (Atul Gawande in a 1998 Slate piece) admitted was the point: cutting off food and medicine to force discontent. `[00:20:48]`

## The cast of characters `[00:24:36]`
- **Fidel Castro** — baby-faced lawyer for Cuba's workers → leader of a failed 1953 attack on the Moncada barracks against the **Batista** dictatorship → prison → exile in Mexico → the revolution. As leader he made education, health, science, and internationalism central; survived 600+ assassination attempts; enemies included Nixon and the Kennedys, friends included Malcolm X and Hugo Chávez. `[00:24:36]`
- **The Kennedys** `[00:28:07]` — JFK and RFK, princeling sons of the bootlegging, Nazi-sympathizing Joseph Kennedy. JFK: a liberal anti-communist with a ghostwritten war-hero bio. RFK: a moralizing former McCarthy aide turned organized-crime prosecutor who, as AG, oversaw the assassination plots against Castro (via the alcoholic CIA officer **William Harvey**, who hated him).
- **Meyer Lansky** `[00:30:25]` — the mob financier who built the pre-revolution Havana casino/hotel empire and dealt directly with Batista; he lost millions when the revolution nationalized everything, and turned to arranging anti-Cuba terrorism and picking Castro's would-be successors.
- **Richard Bissell** `[00:32:33]` — the brilliant, arrogant CIA spymaster (Marshall Plan administrator, U-2 architect) who ran the Guatemala coup and then the **Bay of Pigs**.
- **Celia Sánchez** `[00:34:14]` — the under-credited revolutionary (dismissed as "Castro's secretary") who saved the rebels after their disastrous landing, ran urban sabotage, and archived the revolution's history.
- **Nikita Khrushchev** `[00:36:20]` — the crass-but-clever Soviet leader; the show previews that Moscow was actually *cautious* about Cuba at first (no one sent until late 1959), and the Cuba–USSR relationship had a real ebb and flow.

## The season ahead `[00:38:20]`
- The arc: the **revolution** (and a deep dive back to Spanish colonialism) → the US/CIA/mafia/exile reaction → the **Bay of Pigs** → **Operation Mongoose** → the deepening Cuba–USSR bond → the climax of the **Missile Crisis** ("Caribbean Crisis" to the Soviets, "October Crisis" to the Cubans). Plus extensive **interviews with Cuban guests** woven throughout, and a planned run of bonus episodes. `[00:38:20]`
- Closes (and titles the episode) by teasing the nuclear stakes, and ends on a clip of **Lee Harvey Oswald** introducing himself as a representative of the "Fair Play for Cuba Committee." `[00:44:50]`
