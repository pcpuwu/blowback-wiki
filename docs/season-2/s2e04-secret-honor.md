## Cold open: "who lost Cuba?" `[00:00:00]`
- A 1960 Nixon–Kennedy debate clip arguing over Cuba — establishing how a decade of anti-communism smothered the discourse (in 1960, 22% of Americans lived in poverty, yet the Soviet nuke was on everyone's lips). `[00:00:00]`

## Nixon's obsession & the formal plan `[00:01:33]`
- VP **Nixon** was obsessed with Cuba (an aide nearly struck by lightning running an anti-Castro errand). By May 1960, 72% of Americans viewed Castro unfavorably. Recap: the escalation spiral → **embargo (Oct 1960)** → **Eisenhower severs relations (Jan 3, 1961)** ("there is a limit to what self-respect can endure"). `[00:01:33]`
- **The plan formalizes**: from **WH/4** (Jan 1960) to the **Aug 18, 1960** meeting where Eisenhower approved a **$13M** budget. Dulles's four axes: build exile support, US military training, propaganda, a non-American "standby force." The self-imposed handcuff: **no US military in combat.** The whole playbook was lifted from **Guatemala 1954** (Bissell and Hunt were veterans of it) — "Guatemala 2.0." `[00:05:30]`

## Building the exile army `[00:09:02]`
- **The Frente** (FRD) — the CIA's exile umbrella. Ex-revolutionary **Manuel Artime** was recruited by "**Frank Bender**" (really CIA man Jerry Droller, who spoke no Spanish) and **Howard Hunt** ("Eduardo," a right-wing Watergate-future spook). Both were "weirdos" wildly ill-suited to wrangling Cubans; endless ego-soothing and funding fights (the Frente wanted ~$500k/mo to buy its own arms; got ~$131k). Propaganda via **Radio Swan** (David Atlee Phillips). `[00:09:02]`
- **Guatemala training** `[00:20:26]`: after Mexico balked, training moved to Guatemala (pro-US since 1954, $50M/yr in US aid). The decrepit **Finca Helvetia / "Fort Trax."** The exile force became **Brigade 2506** (named for fallen recruit #2506 — the CIA started serial numbers at 2500 to fake a bigger force). Commander **Pepe San Román**; the brigade was whiter and wealthier than most Cubans. A Nov 1960 coup attempt against Guatemala's Ydígoras was suppressed partly by the exiles; their drunken bad behavior embarrassed everyone. `[00:20:26]`
- **From guerrilla to invasion** `[00:21:43]`: because guerrilla infiltration was achieving little, an **Oct 31, 1960 cable** reframed it as a full **amphibious invasion** (~1,500 men, B-26 bombers, Somoza's Nicaragua airstrip) — one of the hardest military maneuvers there is. `[00:21:43]`
- **The secrecy farce** `[00:34:58]`: despite Bissell's obsession with secrecy, leaks were everywhere — the British FO asked about it in 1959; *The Nation*, a Guatemalan paper, and a **Jan 10, 1961 NYT** story (with a map) exposed the camps; Cuba's UN delegation laid out detailed evidence — all denied as "groundless." (Hunt even got the brigade's training photos *published*.) `[00:34:58]`

## The 1960 election & the mafia `[00:39:44]`
- **JFK out-Cuba'd Nixon**: briefed by Dulles on the nascent operation, Kennedy ran to Nixon's right ("the Iron Curtain is 90 miles off our coast," calling for aid to anti-Castro exiles) — and Nixon, unable to reveal the secret plan, looked soft. Anti-communism ran in the Kennedy family (RFK once worked for McCarthy). The Illinois **mob (Giancana)** reportedly helped JFK win. He won (by ~300k votes), and kept Dulles. `[00:39:44]`
- **The poison plot** `[00:25:52]`: the assassination program had crystallized into a CIA–mafia contract — **Giancana**'s associates to poison Castro (Giancana boasted of it to the McGuire sisters). The CIA's lab tested poison pills and botulinum cigars on monkeys. `[00:25:52]`

## Fidel in Harlem (Sept 1960) `[00:53:04]`
- For the UN General Assembly, the now-demonized Fidel was confined to Manhattan and beset by lurid press. After the Shelburne Hotel demanded a $20,000 deposit, Fidel decamped to the **Hotel Theresa in Harlem** — an offer relayed via **Malcolm X** (through the Fair Play for Cuba Committee). Thousands filled the streets (Maya Angelou among them). `[00:53:04]`
- **Malcolm X & Fidel** `[01:00:29]`: Malcolm called Fidel "the only white person I have really liked"; their exchange on propaganda ("your people… understand"). Historian Rosemary Mealy frames it as a continuation of a century of Black–Cuban solidarity. **Khrushchev** insisted on visiting Harlem too (over US objections — the US hated drawing the international spotlight to *northern* racism); he and Fidel met (20 minutes, "highly symbolic"). When Eisenhower pointedly excluded Fidel from a Latin American lunch, Fidel threw a steak-and-beer lunch for the Theresa's Black staff. He also met Lumumba, Nasser, Nkrumah, Nehru, and Kim Il-sung — a node of Third World / Black internationalism. `[01:00:29]`

## JFK takes the plan `[00:45:00]`
- **Alliance for Progress** (March 1961) — development aid as an anti-communism "sword" (far less than the Marshall Plan). `[00:49:32]`
- JFK got his first full invasion briefing in **late Jan 1961** and signed off; he rejected the CIA's **Trinidad plan** as too "**noisy**" (too obviously American) and the landing was shifted ~100 miles west to swampy **Playa Girón** — a month before the invasion. `[00:49:32]`
- **The CRC** (Cuban Revolutionary Council, March 1961) replaced the Frente — fully pliant to the CIA after the ultimatum "fall in line or it's all off." `[01:11:02]`
- **The pills are delivered** `[01:13:38]`: **mid-March 1961**, in Miami for a heavyweight fight, CIA handler O'Connell passed three botulinum capsules to Roselli — to reach an asset *inside Castro's office* — timed for the invasion. `[01:13:38]`
